Methodology

Each of the 20 priority markets is scored 1–10 from four factors. The three continuous factors are min-max normalized across the 20 states, then weighted:

  • Avg Infrastructure Density (35%) — the mean per-substation infrastructure- density score (1–10) across the state's interconnection-queue substations. Measures the typical quality of grid interconnection points.
  • Infrastructure Depth (25%) — a normalized count of the state's STRONG and MODERATE density substations (STRONG weighted 2×). Measures grid breadth, so a large state with distributed infrastructure is not penalized for lacking a single dominant hub. (Replaces a raw STRONG-only count, which zeroed out distributed-grid states.)
  • Weighted Load Growth (25%) — utility retail-sales year-over-year growth, weighted by load; non-organic reporting outliers (new-entrant retailers, >100%) are excluded.
  • Competitive Market (15%) — data-center market competition, not grid capacity. A "Competitive Market" is a built-out market with more rivals for sites and power; less-developed markets score higher (open runway). This factor never indicates a thin grid — grid is measured entirely by the two factors above.

"Competitive Market" = market-competition pressure, not absence of grid infrastructure. A state can be a Competitive Market and still have deep, high-quality grid (e.g., Virginia, California).

This report is a market screening tool. It identifies power-advantaged markets for further diligence. It does not replace utility service availability confirmation.

Data Sources & Vintage

  • Interconnection queue — live ISO/RTO interconnection-queue data (PJM, MISO, ERCOT, CAISO, SPP, NYISO, ISO-NE, Southeast); refreshed weekly.
  • Grid infrastructure — EIA-860 ( vintage): plant-level grid-interconnection voltage and location.
  • Utility load — EIA-861 ( vintage): utility retail sales and year-over-year load growth.
