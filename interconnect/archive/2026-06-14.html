Weekly intelligence on power procurement, interconnection queues, and data center development — for teams building at the grid edge.

Week of June 14, 2026 · Vol. 1, No. 2
NexPhaseAdvisory.net

The data center development pipeline shows no signs of discipline—this week alone, Stark Power acquired 5.6GW of development assets for ~$140M, Hut 8 priced a $4.25B note offering to fund a pre-leased 352MW Texas campus, and new projects surfaced from Virginia to Illinois to Spain, collectively representing tens of gigawatts of demand still searching for interconnection queue position and dispatchable capacity. The geographic spread matters: developers are pressing into constrained markets like PJM-adjacent Virginia and ERCOT coastal Texas, while others are betting on greenfield positions in Illinois and Mississippi where regulatory clarity remains unresolved. Cipher Mining's move to site 500MW near a retired coal plant outside Waco is the clearest signal yet that stranded grid infrastructure is now a competitive siting advantage, not a consolation prize. For power developers and IPPs, the capital formation side—Hut 8's debt raise, Stark's equity round, Magnora's Oslo listing—confirms that offtake-backed data center load is being treated as investable infrastructure, compressing the window to lock in power supply agreements before these projects harden their stack.

This Week's Signals
ERCOT Nueces County, TX · Demand Signal

Beacon Point AI Eyes Massive Data Center Build in Nueces County

Beacon Point AI is planning a large-scale data center development in Nueces County, Texas. The project signals continued hyperscale-adjacent demand pressure on ERCOT's coastal South zone. Nueces County sits near Corpus Christi, an area with existing industrial load and transmission infrastructure.

The Angle Nueces County is an emerging ERCOT demand node — developers should watch for transmission constraint signals in the South zone and engage early with AEP Texas on interconnection capacity before the queue fills.
ERCOT Texas (Statewide) · M&A

Stark Power Acquires Sagebrush's 5.6GW DC Portfolio for ~$140M

Stark Power has acquired Sagebrush's 5.6GW data center development portfolio, raising approximately $140 million to fund the transaction. The deal represents one of the largest portfolio acquisitions of pre-development data center capacity in recent memory. The scale suggests Stark is positioning for long-term hyperscaler offtake across multiple Texas sites.

The Angle A 5.6GW pipeline acquisition signals that institutional capital is consolidating development rights ahead of demand — power sellers and co-located generation developers should engage Stark Power sites as priority interconnection and PPA targets.
PJM Goochland County, VA · Demand Signal

Tract Proposes Large Data Center Campus Near West Creek Corridor

Tract is proposing a large data center campus in Goochland County, Virginia, adjacent to the established West Creek technology corridor. The West Creek area is one of the most data center-dense corridors in the world and already faces significant transmission congestion. This addition would further stress Dominion Energy Virginia's local transmission network.

The Angle Goochland County is immediately west of the saturated Ashburn/West Creek corridor — developers must conduct rigorous network upgrade studies upfront as interconnection costs and timelines in this PJM zone have escalated sharply.
MISO Mississippi · Regulatory

Compass Datacenters Advances Mississippi Plans Despite Regulatory Setback

Compass Datacenters is pressing forward with its Mississippi data center development despite the state declining to issue a regulatory ruling on the project. The regulatory ambiguity introduces permitting and utility cost-recovery uncertainty. Mississippi remains a relatively untested data center market with limited precedent for large load interconnections.

The Angle The absence of a regulatory ruling could complicate utility cost-recovery for grid upgrades — developers entering Mississippi should negotiate explicit interconnection and rate treatment terms directly with the serving utility before breaking ground.
MISO Christian County, IL · Demand Signal

Eagle Rock Plans 1GW Illinois Data Center, Construction from 2029

Eagle Rock has proposed a 1GW data center in Christian County, Illinois, with construction not expected to begin until 2029–2030. The long lead time suggests the project is in early-stage site control and interconnection positioning. MISO Illinois is a competitive interconnection zone with a heavily backlogged generator queue.

The Angle The 2029–2030 start date gives developers a window to monitor queue progression, but the 1GW scale means transmission upgrade costs could be substantial — early MISO cluster study engagement is essential to avoid being stranded behind larger generation projects.
SPP Topeka, KS · Regulatory

Evergy Explores Utility-Led Data Center Siting in Topeka

Evergy is actively exploring siting a data center in Topeka, Kansas, marking a notable shift toward utility-led demand development in the SPP footprint. Utilities self-developing or courting data center load directly signals a tightening competitive environment for independent developers. Kansas has historically been underrepresented in data center investment relative to its grid capacity.

The Angle Utility-driven siting typically means preferential interconnection treatment and favorable rate structures — third-party developers should monitor whether Evergy's involvement creates a first-mover regulatory advantage that crowds out independent projects in the Topeka area.
ERCOT Waco, TX · Demand Signal

Cipher Mining Plans 500MW Data Center at Retired Coal Plant Site

Cipher Mining is developing a 500MW data center campus near a retired coal plant outside Waco, Texas, leveraging existing substation and transmission infrastructure. Brownfield coal-to-data-center conversions are gaining traction in ERCOT as a strategy to bypass lengthy new interconnection queues. The Waco area sits in ERCOT's Central zone with reasonable grid access.

The Angle Retired plant infrastructure reuse is one of the fastest paths to large-scale interconnection in ERCOT right now — developers and investors should prioritize brownfield site identification as greenfield interconnection timelines continue to stretch.
NYISO Albany, NY · Demand Signal

Prime Companies Unveils $2.8B Kenwood Tech Campus in Albany Area

Prime Companies has announced a $2.8 billion Kenwood technology campus and data center plan in the Albany, New York metropolitan area. The Capital Region has seen growing interest from data center developers attracted by competitive power costs and proximity to financial and government markets. NYISO Zone F transmission capacity will be a key constraint to monitor.

The Angle Albany's data center market is early-stage with real upside, but NYISO interconnection in Zone F requires careful load flow analysis — developers should engage National Grid early and assess whether local generation co-location can reduce interconnection upgrade exposure.
ERCOT Texas · M&A

Hut 8 Prices $4.25B Note to Fund 352MW Hyperscaler-Leased Texas Campus

Hut 8 has priced a $4.25 billion note offering to finance a 352MW Texas data center that is pre-leased to an undisclosed hyperscaler. The financing structure demonstrates growing capital market confidence in contracted data center cash flows as collateral. Pre-leased assets at this scale are attracting investment-grade-style debt pricing.

The Angle The $4.25B raise against 352MW of contracted hyperscaler capacity sets a new benchmark for data center debt financing in ERCOT — power and infrastructure investors should note that pre-lease structures are now unlocking non-dilutive capital at scale, reshaping how projects are capitalized.
