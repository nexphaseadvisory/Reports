Week of June 9, 2026 · Vol. 1, No. 2
NexPhaseAdvisory.net

Capital formation and site control dominated the week's deal flow, with Stark Power's $140M acquisition of Sagebrush's 5.6GW development portfolio and Hut 8's $4.25B note offering for a pre-leased 352MW Texas campus signaling that the financing structures underwriting hyperscale demand are maturing fast. The site selection map is expanding beyond Tier 1 power markets — Nueces County, Christian County (Illinois), Albany, Mississippi, and outer Adelaide all surfaced as active fronts — while Cipher Mining's move to a retired coal site near Waco underscores how developers are arbitraging stranded grid infrastructure to compress interconnection timelines. Evergy's apparent willingness to sponsor data center development directly in Topeka is a notable utility posture shift worth watching, and Jane Street self-developing its own facility is a reminder that end-user vertical integration is no longer confined to hyperscalers. For developers and IPPs, the week reinforces a single operational reality: the constraint is no longer capital or demand, it's permitted land with viable grid access and a clear path to power delivery.

This Week's Signals
ERCOT Nueces County, TX · Demand Signal

Beacon Point AI Eyes Massive Data Center in Nueces County

Beacon Point AI is planning a large-scale data center development in Nueces County, Texas. The project signals continued hyperscale-adjacent demand concentration along the Texas Gulf Coast. No MW figure has been publicly disclosed yet.

The Angle Nueces County sits in ERCOT's South load zone with coastal transmission constraints — developers here should model congestion risk carefully and engage early with AEP Texas on interconnection capacity.
ERCOT Texas (statewide) · M&A

Stark Power Acquires 5.6GW Sagebrush Data Center Portfolio

Stark Power has acquired Sagebrush's 5.6GW data center development portfolio, raising approximately $140M to fund the transaction. This is one of the largest portfolio acquisitions in the data center development space in recent memory. The deal consolidates a significant pipeline of ERCOT-focused load-side interconnection rights.

The Angle 5.6GW of queued load in a single transaction reshapes the competitive landscape for power origination in ERCOT — offtakers and generators should reassess their pipeline assumptions and watch how Stark Power stages these projects.
MISO Mississippi · Regulatory

Compass Datacenters Pushes Forward Despite Mississippi Regulatory Setback

Compass Datacenters is advancing its Mississippi data center development despite the state declining to issue a regulatory ruling on the project. This creates an unusual situation where development is proceeding without formal state regulatory clarity. The move suggests the developer believes the project can progress under existing frameworks.

The Angle Operating without a regulatory ruling introduces permitting and utility coordination risk — due diligence on power delivery agreements and interconnection status is critical before any capital commitment here.
MISO Christian County, IL · Demand Signal

Eagle Rock Proposes 1GW Illinois Data Center, Construction Deferred to 2029

Eagle Rock has proposed a 1GW data center in Christian County, Illinois, with construction not expected to begin until 2029–2030. The long lead time suggests this is an early-stage land and queue positioning play. Christian County is in central Illinois within MISO's footprint.

The Angle A 2029 construction start means interconnection queue positioning and site control matter now — developers and landowners in central Illinois MISO territory should evaluate whether this signals a broader cluster forming in the region.
SPP Topeka, KS · Demand Signal

Evergy Explores Utility-Led Data Center Development in Topeka

Evergy is exploring siting a data center in Topeka, Kansas, representing a notable instance of utility-led data center development activity. Utilities self-developing or attracting anchor data center tenants is an emerging trend that blurs traditional load-serving roles. Topeka sits within Evergy's regulated SPP service territory.

The Angle Utility-sponsored data center development can accelerate grid integration and reduce interconnection friction — but it also raises questions about rate base treatment and competitive neutrality that regulators and independent developers should monitor closely.
ERCOT Waco, TX · Demand Signal

Cipher Mining Targets 500MW Campus at Retired Coal Site Near Waco

Cipher Mining plans a 500MW data center campus near a retired coal plant outside Waco, Texas, leveraging existing grid infrastructure at the brownfield site. Reuse of retired generation sites is an increasingly common strategy to access transmission capacity without new queue entry. The Waco area sits in ERCOT's North load zone.

The Angle Brownfield coal sites offer real transmission infrastructure advantages, but developers must validate substation condition, remaining asset life, and ERCOT's view on load interconnection at these points before underwriting the cost savings.
NYISO Albany, NY · Demand Signal

Prime Companies Unveils $2.8B Tech Campus and Data Center Near Albany

Developer Prime Companies has unveiled a $2.8 billion Kenwood tech campus and data center plan in the Albany area of New York. The project represents one of the larger announced data center investments in Upstate New York in recent years. Albany sits within NYISO Zone F, which has historically seen less data center saturation than the New York City metro.

The Angle Zone F offers relatively lower congestion costs and proximity to hydropower-heavy generation mix — a compelling clean-energy narrative for offtakers, though NYISO's interconnection process and local permitting in Albany County will be key gating factors.
ERCOT Texas (West) · M&A

Hut 8 Raises $4.25B to Fund 352MW Pre-Leased Texas Data Center

Hut 8 has priced a $4.25 billion note offering to finance a 352MW Texas data center that is already pre-leased to an undisclosed hyperscaler. The transaction demonstrates strong capital market appetite for contracted data center cash flows in ERCOT. The hyperscaler identity has not been disclosed, adding some counterparty opacity.

The Angle A pre-leased hyperscaler deal at this scale with note financing closed signals that institutional debt markets are fully open for contracted Texas data center projects — comparable assets with signed offtake should be actively exploring similar capital structures.
ERCOT Childress, TX · Demand Signal

IREN Expands Childress Data Center, Highlights Low Water Footprint

IREN is expanding its data center operation in Childress, Texas, creating local jobs and emphasizing the project's limited water consumption. Childress is located in the Texas Panhandle, a region with historically constrained water resources, making the low-water angle strategically important. The expansion signals continued scaling of IREN's Texas infrastructure footprint.

The Angle Water availability is becoming a real site selection constraint in West Texas — IREN's low-water positioning could differentiate their sites in regulatory and community approval processes as scrutiny on data center resource use intensifies.
