ERCOT's Batch Zero Signals a New Era for Large-Load Reviews

Texas's new centralized interconnection framework arrives just as a $2B transmission dispute and surging data-center filings expose the limits of the existing process.

ERCOT approved its Batch Zero framework this week, centralizing and streamlining large-load interconnection reviews in direct response to AI and data-center demand overwhelming the queue. The timing is pointed: a separate dispute at the PUC over the $2B, 765-kV Permian Basin transmission line—where landowners are seeking a pause after route changes tied to ERCOT grid expansion triggered improper-notice claims—illustrates exactly the kind of downstream friction that accelerated queue reform is meant to outrun. DataBank's concurrent filing for a 200MW campus outside Atlanta is a reminder that the large-load pressure driving Texas's policy shift is regional, not local.

Batch Zero matters because it converts interconnection review from a first-come, first-served queue into a coordinated, bulk-intake process, giving ERCOT visibility into cumulative load additions before individual studies propagate conflicting assumptions through the system. The Permian Basin line dispute underscores the stakes: transmission infrastructure committed to serve new load is now legally contested mid-build, and any procedural delay on a 765-kV project ripples across reliability planning for the entire west Texas corridor. For hyperscalers and IPPs simultaneously negotiating large-load service agreements in ERCOT, queue position just became less important than cohort timing under the new batch structure.

Watch whether ERCOT publishes intake windows and MW caps for subsequent batches, which will effectively set the pace of large-load additions the grid operator is willing to absorb per cycle. The Oncor/LCRA transmission case at the PUC is the near-term pressure test: if landowner objections succeed in pausing that route, the transmission backbone underpinning future batch approvals faces a credibility gap. Nexus DC's ~500,000 sq ft Dallas filing targeting go-live next year will be an early indicator of whether developers believe Batch Zero timelines are compatible with their commercial schedules.

PPA & Financing

Market Pricing Context

Permanent Power Company's $600M construction financing for a 250MW/600MWh solar-plus-storage project in California implies a roughly $2.40/W all-in capital intensity benchmark for paired utility-scale assets in CAISO — a useful datapoint as lenders price similar structures elsewhere. The concurrent wave of behind-the-meter natural gas deployments (Z Squared's 150MW immersion-cooled Arkansas campus, Valley Electric's Nevada gas plant request) signals that developers are actively pricing the optionality of bypassing interconnection queues against the fuel-cost and offtake risk of merchant gas, with no grid-tied PPA required. Sabanci's undisclosed-price PPA with Meta in ERCOT reinforces that hyperscaler offtake continues to clear at terms that support project finance, even as ERCOT's new Batch Zero framework introduces procedural uncertainty on interconnection timelines that lenders will need to underwrite.
