Microsoft breaks ground on 17-building data center campus in La Porte, Indiana

Developers and investors should treat this as a leading indicator of Ameren Missouri's grid capacity and interconnection queue dynamics, warranting early site and utility engagement before congestion follows capital.

ERCOT approves Batch Zero framework to centralize and streamline large-load interconnection reviews amid surging AI and data center demand in Texas.
ERCOT's Batch Zero framework shifts large-load interconnection reviews from a first-come, first-served queue to a centralized, batched evaluation process, reducing speculative queue congestion that has historically delayed serious projects. For data center developers and CRE investors, this signals faster, more predictable timelines for high-MW site approvals in Texas—but also heightened competition, as projects will now be evaluated collectively rather than sequentially.
