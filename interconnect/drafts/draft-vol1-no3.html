Interconnection

Nuclear and Coal-CCS Bids Signal PJM's Firm-Capacity Reckoning

Two large dispatchable projects targeting PJM — an SMR campus and a coal-with-CCS greenfield — reflect developer conviction that the region will reward firm, queue-navigable capacity above all else.

Elementl Power is advancing a project of up to 1,500 MW of small modular reactor capacity along the Ohio River roughly 100 miles southeast of Columbus, partnering with GE Vernova Hitachi, while TerraSpark Energy is developing a 1,600-MW greenfield coal-plus-CCS campus in West Virginia targeting a 2030 startup, backed by $18.5 million in DOE funding. Both projects sit inside PJM's footprint and represent a deliberate bet on dispatchable, around-the-clock generation at a scale that wind and solar paired with storage cannot yet match on a firm-capacity basis. The simultaneous emergence of these two very different technology paths — advanced nuclear and carbon-captured coal — underscores how wide the field remains for developers willing to absorb long development timelines.

The significance is interconnection queue positioning: projects of this size pursuing firm interconnection in PJM face a queue that has become structurally hostile to large thermal additions in terms of study cycles and cost allocation, making early-mover queue filings and site control around existing transmission infrastructure critical differentiators. The DOE funding behind TerraSpark, while modest relative to a 1,600-MW build, signals federal appetite to validate coal-CCS as a grid reliability tool, which carries weight in PJM capacity market proceedings and may influence how FERC evaluates interconnection cost allocations for carbon-capture retrofits. For the Elementl SMR project, GE Vernova Hitachi's BWRX-300 design is one of the few SMR platforms with an active NRC licensing track, giving it a credible path to a definitive commercial operation date.

Watch whether either project files a formal interconnection request with PJM's post-FERC Order 2023 cluster process in the next two quarters — that filing, and the associated network upgrade cost allocation it triggers, will be the first hard test of whether these projects can survive the queue economics that have killed comparably sized thermal proposals in recent cycles. The 2030 target on the TerraSpark coal-CCS campus is particularly load-bearing: it implies a construction start inside three years on a technology with no operating U.S. precedent at utility scale. Data-center load growth across MISO and PJM, visible in Microsoft's LaPorte groundbreaking and Amazon and Google's Missouri commitments this week, only tightens the timeline pressure on any project claiming to serve firm baseload demand by decade's end.

Campus Announcements — Scale & Power Implications

Elementl Power Plans 1.5 GW SMR Nuclear Project in Ohio
Elementl Power and GE Vernova Hitachi are developing up to 1,500 MW of small modular reactor nuclear capacity along the Ohio River, roughly 100 miles southeast of Columbus, in PJM territory.

The project represents one of the largest announced SMR commitments in PJM, siting along an established industrial corridor near Columbus.

TerraSpark Eyes 1.6 GW Coal-Plus-CCS Campus in West Virginia
TerraSpark Energy is developing a 1,600 MW greenfield coal-with-carbon-capture campus in West Virginia, backed by $18.5 million in DOE funding and targeting a 2030 startup.

The PJM-region project would add significant dispatchable capacity to the grid if the 2030 in-service date is met.

Meta Signs 298 MW Solar PPA With RWE in ERCOT
Meta has contracted 298 MW of solar power from an RWE project in Texas under a power purchase agreement, expanding both companies' renewable footprint in ERCOT.

The deal adds to the growing volume of hyperscaler-driven renewable offtake agreements shaping ERCOT's generation queue.

Microsoft Breaks Ground on 17-Building Campus in La Porte, Indiana
Microsoft has broken ground on a 17-building hyperscale data center campus in La Porte, Indiana, representing a major new load addition in MISO.

The campus scale underscores the accelerating pace of hyperscale buildout across MISO's Indiana footprint.

PPA & Financing

Market Pricing Context

Meta's 298 MW solar PPA with RWE in ERCOT is the clearest utility-scale pricing benchmark this week, reflecting continued hyperscaler appetite for long-tenor contracted renewables in a market where merchant risk remains elevated. The deal signals that even in liquid spot markets like ERCOT, large offtakers are still willing to pay a fixed-price premium for volume certainty and additionality optics. The implication for PPA desks: hyperscaler credit is effectively subsidizing developer returns in competitive solar markets, compressing the risk premium that merchant projects would otherwise require to clear financing.
