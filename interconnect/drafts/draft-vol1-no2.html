Week of June 9, 2026 · Vol. 1, No. 2
NexPhaseAdvisory.net

Capital formation and site control dominated this week's data center pipeline activity, with Stark Power's $140M acquisition of Sagebrush's 5.6GW portfolio and Hut 8's $4.25B note offering for a pre-leased Texas campus signaling that institutional money is moving aggressively to lock in development optionality before interconnection queues tighten further. The geographic spread of new announcements—Nueces County, Christian County, Albany, Mississippi, Kansas, and South Australia—underscores that developers are scouring secondary markets for grid capacity and political headroom that frontline markets can no longer reliably offer. Cipher Mining's move to repurpose infrastructure near a retired Waco coal plant is a template worth watching: brownfield grid assets are becoming a genuine competitive moat as greenfield interconnection timelines stretch. Compass Datacenters pressing forward in Mississippi without a regulatory ruling, and Eagle Rock penciling 2029–2030 construction starts in Illinois, are both reminders that the pipeline looks robust on paper but execution risk—permitting, offtake, and utility alignment—remains the variable that separates announced megawatts from energized ones.

This Week's Signals
ERCOT Nueces County, TX · Demand Signal

Beacon Point AI Eyes Massive Data Center in Nueces County

Beacon Point AI is planning a large-scale data center development in Nueces County, Texas. The project signals continued hyperscale-adjacent demand pressure on the ERCOT coastal zone. Specific MW figures have not been disclosed publicly.

The Angle Nueces County sits near Corpus Christi load zone with constrained transmission — early interconnection queue positioning is critical before this demand wave hits ERCOT's coastal nodes.
ERCOT Texas (statewide) · M&A

Stark Power Acquires Sagebrush 5.6GW DC Portfolio for $140M

Stark Power has acquired Sagebrush's 5.6GW data center development portfolio, raising approximately $140M to fund the transaction. The deal represents one of the larger portfolio acquisitions of pre-development data center assets in ERCOT. Portfolio site locations across Texas have not been fully disclosed.

The Angle A 5.6GW block of optioned sites and queue positions changing hands signals that early-stage ERCOT DC land and interconnection rights are being capitalized at scale — developers holding similar assets should reassess their portfolio valuations now.
MISO Mississippi · Regulatory

Compass Pushes Mississippi Data Center Despite Regulatory Silence

Compass Datacenters is advancing its Mississippi data center project even after the state declined to issue a regulatory ruling on the development. The absence of a ruling creates ambiguity around utility cost recovery and interconnection obligations. This posture suggests the developer is betting on de facto approval through inaction.

The Angle Regulatory gray zones in MISO South can stall construction financing — power purchase and interconnection structures need to be airtight before committing capital in markets where state regulators are non-committal.
MISO Christian County, IL · Demand Signal

Eagle Rock Plans 1GW Illinois Data Center, Construction Starting 2029

Eagle Rock is proposing a 1GW data center in Christian County, Illinois, with construction not expected to begin until 2029–2030. The long lead time suggests the project is in early-stage site control and interconnection queue positioning. Christian County sits in central Illinois within MISO's footprint.

The Angle A 2029 start date means MISO queue cycle timing matters enormously here — securing a Definitive Planning Phase position now could be the difference between project viability and a decade-long wait.
SPP Topeka, KS · Demand Signal

Evergy Explores Utility-Led Data Center Siting in Topeka

Evergy, the Kansas-based regulated utility, is exploring siting a data center in Topeka, signaling a shift toward utility-led data center development activity. This model — where the utility itself drives load attraction — is an emerging dynamic in SPP territory. If successful, it could reshape how large loads are connected and rate-based in the region.

The Angle Utility-sponsored DC development in SPP can compress interconnection timelines and offer more predictable cost structures — worth monitoring as a partnership or co-location pathway for developers struggling with queue congestion.
ERCOT Waco, TX · Demand Signal

Cipher Mining Plans 500MW Campus Near Retired Waco Coal Plant

Cipher Mining is planning a 500MW data center campus near a retired coal plant outside Waco, Texas, leveraging existing grid infrastructure at the brownfield site. Repurposing retired generation infrastructure provides transmission access and substation assets that can significantly reduce interconnection costs and timelines. The Waco area sits in the ERCOT North load zone.

The Angle Brownfield-to-data-center conversions at retired thermal sites are becoming a competitive playbook in ERCOT — the existing substation and transmission assets can cut 18–24 months off a greenfield interconnection schedule.
NYISO Albany, NY · Demand Signal

Prime Companies Unveils $2.8B Kenwood Tech Campus Near Albany

Developer Prime Companies has announced a $2.8 billion Kenwood tech campus and data center plan in the Albany area of New York. The project would represent a significant load addition to NYISO Zone F. Albany's proximity to state government and academic institutions positions it as an emerging upstate tech corridor.

The Angle NYISO Zone F has historically had more transmission headroom than Zones J and K — but a $2.8B campus will test local distribution capacity and may trigger significant network upgrade costs that need to be priced into project economics.
ERCOT Texas · M&A

Hut 8 Prices $4.25B Note to Fund 352MW Hyperscaler-Leased Campus

Hut 8 has priced a $4.25 billion note offering to fund a 352MW Texas data center that is pre-leased to an undisclosed hyperscaler. The capital raise is one of the largest single-project debt financings for a data center in ERCOT to date. The identity of the hyperscaler tenant has not been disclosed, though the pre-lease structure de-risks the revenue profile significantly.

The Angle Pre-leased hyperscaler deals at this scale are setting new benchmarks for ERCOT project financing — developers without anchor tenants will face a widening cost-of-capital gap as lenders increasingly expect credit-backed offtake before committing.
ERCOT Childress, TX · Demand Signal

IREN Expands Childress Data Center with Low Water Use Focus

IREN is announcing an expansion of its Childress, Texas data center, creating local jobs and emphasizing limited water consumption in its operational profile. Childress is located in the ERCOT North zone in the Texas Panhandle region. The water-use messaging reflects growing scrutiny from Texas municipalities and regulators around data center resource consumption.

The Angle IREN's proactive water-use positioning in Childress is a signal that community and regulatory optics are becoming a site-selection factor in ERCOT — developers should bake water impact assessments into early permitting strategy to avoid local opposition.
