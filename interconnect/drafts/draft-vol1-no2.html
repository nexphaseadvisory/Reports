Week of June 10, 2026 · Vol. 1, No. 2
NexPhaseAdvisory.net

Capacity announcements this week span three continents and aggregate well north of 9GW in stated development pipeline, but the more telling signal is structural: capital is pooling in formats that would have looked unusual eighteen months ago—a quant firm self-financing its own facility, a utility exploring developer-side land control in Topeka, a crypto miner repurposing retired coal infrastructure near Waco, and a $4.25bn note offering backstopped by a single undisclosed hyperscaler lease. Stark Power's acquisition of Sagebrush's 5.6GW portfolio for roughly $140M underscores how cheaply optionality is still trading relative to the headline numbers attached to it, while Compass pressing forward in Mississippi without a regulatory ruling and Eagle Rock projecting a 2029–2030 shovel date in Illinois are reminders that announced MW and deliverable MW remain very different commodities. For developers and IPPs, the practical read is that interconnection position, existing infrastructure, and regulatory certainty are commanding real premiums—projects that lack any one of those three are quietly becoming someone else's problem.

This Week's Signals
ERCOT Nueces County, TX · Demand Signal

Beacon Point AI Eyes Massive Data Center in Nueces County

Beacon Point AI is planning a large-scale data center development in Nueces County, Texas. The project adds to a growing pipeline of hyperscale load seeking interconnection in South Texas. Nueces County sits within ERCOT's coastal region, where transmission constraints and renewable integration are active concerns.

The Angle South Texas is becoming a hotspot for AI-driven load growth; developers should monitor ERCOT transmission congestion in the Corpus Christi load zone and price basis risk accordingly.
ERCOT Texas (statewide) · M&A

Stark Power Acquires Sagebrush's 5.6GW Data Center Portfolio

Stark Power has acquired Sagebrush's 5.6GW data center development portfolio, raising approximately $140M to fund the transaction. The deal represents one of the largest portfolio acquisitions of development-stage data center assets in ERCOT. The move signals continued institutional appetite for pre-interconnection data center land positions.

The Angle A 5.6GW portfolio trade validates that early-stage queue positions and site control in ERCOT carry real option value — those still holding similar assets should reassess book value and inbound interest.
MISO Mississippi · Regulatory

Compass Advances Mississippi Data Center Despite Regulatory Setback

Compass Datacenters is pressing forward with its Mississippi data center plans even after the state declined to issue a regulatory ruling on the project. The absence of a regulatory determination creates uncertainty around utility cost recovery and rate treatment for large load. This situation is emblematic of friction between hyperscale load growth timelines and state utility commission processes.

The Angle Proceeding without regulatory clarity on cost recovery is a meaningful risk signal — power purchase terms and utility agreements for this project could face legal challenge or renegotiation down the line.
MISO Christian County, IL · Demand Signal

Eagle Rock Proposes 1GW Illinois Data Center, Construction by 2029

Eagle Rock has proposed a 1GW data center in Christian County, Illinois, with construction not expected to begin until 2029–2030. The long lead time suggests this is an early-stage site control and interconnection positioning play. Christian County is in central Illinois, a region with existing MISO transmission infrastructure and proximity to wind resources.

The Angle The 2029–2030 construction window means queue position and land optionality matter more than near-term power contracting — but locking in long-dated renewable PPAs now could be advantageous given current pricing.
SPP Topeka, KS · Demand Signal

Evergy Explores Utility-Led Data Center Siting in Topeka

Evergy is actively exploring siting a data center in Topeka, Kansas, representing an unusual utility-led approach to data center development. Utilities self-developing or facilitating data center load signals a shift in how regulated entities are approaching the AI infrastructure buildout. Kansas sits within SPP territory, where wind generation is abundant and capacity markets are evolving.

The Angle Utility-sponsored data center development in SPP is a novel dynamic — it could compress margins for independent developers but also signals that regulated cost recovery pathways may be available for infrastructure co-locating with Evergy assets.
ERCOT Waco, TX · Demand Signal

Cipher Mining Targets 500MW Campus Near Retired Coal Plant

Cipher Mining is planning a 500MW data center campus near a retired coal plant outside Waco, Texas, leveraging the site's existing grid infrastructure and transmission interconnection. Brownfield coal plant repurposing is an increasingly common strategy for accelerating interconnection timelines in ERCOT. The Waco area sits in ERCOT's North load zone.

The Angle Retired coal plant sites with intact substation infrastructure are among the fastest paths to large-scale load interconnection in ERCOT — this playbook is being replicated across the state and competition for such sites is intensifying.
NYISO Albany, NY · Demand Signal

$2.8B Kenwood Tech Campus Unveiled in Albany Region

Prime Companies has unveiled a $2.8B tech campus and data center plan in the Albany area of New York. The project adds significant prospective load to NYISO Zone F, which has historically been less congested than New York City zones. Albany's proximity to hydropower and a growing semiconductor and tech corridor enhances its appeal for compute infrastructure.

The Angle Zone F load additions of this scale could tighten capacity and energy prices in upstate New York — developers and offtakers should watch NYISO's next capacity market auctions for early price signals.
ERCOT Texas · M&A

Hut 8 Prices $4.25B Note to Fund 352MW Pre-Leased Texas Campus

Hut 8 has priced a $4.25B note offering to finance a 352MW Texas data center that is pre-leased to an undisclosed hyperscaler. The deal demonstrates strong capital market appetite for fully contracted data center assets in ERCOT. Pre-lease to a hyperscaler substantially de-risks the project from a revenue standpoint, supporting the large debt raise.

The Angle Hyperscaler pre-leases at this scale are the gold standard for debt financing in the current market — developers without anchor tenants will face meaningfully higher capital costs and should prioritize offtake negotiations before going to market.
