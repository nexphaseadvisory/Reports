Week of June 9, 2026 · Vol. 1, No. 2
NexPhaseAdvisory.net

Capital formation for data center capacity accelerated on multiple fronts this week, with Stark Power's $140M acquisition of Sagebrush's 5.6GW portfolio and Hut 8's $4.25B note offering for a hyperscaler-pre-leased Texas campus signaling that institutional money is moving decisively from pipeline to balance sheet. The geographic spread of announced projects—Nueces County, Christian County, Mississippi, Albany, Adelaide—confirms that developers are no longer anchoring solely to Tier 1 interconnection queues but are actively hunting stranded grid infrastructure, favorable regulatory environments, and utility-led offtake structures. Cipher Mining's play on a retired coal plant outside Waco is emblematic of a broader strategy: inherited transmission capacity is now a primary site selection criterion, not a secondary consideration. Regulators and utilities are entering the frame as active participants rather than gatekeepers, with Evergy's Topeka exploration and Compass's decision to advance in Mississippi despite a declined ruling both illustrating that developers are pricing regulatory friction as a manageable variable. Executives tracking load interconnection timelines should note that the Eagle Rock Illinois proposal—1GW with a 2029–2030 construction start—reflects the queue reality that is quietly shaping the true delivery schedule behind today's headline announcements.

This Week's Signals
ERCOT Nueces County, TX · Demand Signal

Beacon Point AI Eyes Massive Nueces County Data Center

Beacon Point AI has announced plans for a large-scale data center development in Nueces County, Texas. The project adds to a growing wave of hyperscale and AI-driven load pursuing ERCOT interconnection along the Gulf Coast corridor. Specific MW figures have not yet been disclosed publicly.

The Angle Nueces County sits in a congested ERCOT South zone with constrained transmission — early interconnection queue positioning and transmission study engagement are critical before site control is locked.
PJM Multi-region (Sagebrush Portfolio) · M&A

Stark Power Acquires 5.6GW Sagebrush DC Portfolio for ~$140M

Stark Power has acquired Sagebrush's 5.6GW data center development portfolio, raising approximately $140 million to fund the transaction. The deal represents one of the larger bulk data center pipeline acquisitions in recent memory, signaling strong investor appetite for pre-developed capacity. Portfolio locations span multiple ISOs, though PJM assets are likely core given Sagebrush's historical footprint.

The Angle Portfolio deals of this scale compress available shovel-ready sites — developers and power marketers should accelerate due diligence on any remaining Sagebrush-adjacent queue positions before they get absorbed or repriced.
MISO Mississippi · Regulatory

Compass Datacenters Advances Mississippi Project Despite Regulatory Gap

Compass Datacenters is pushing forward with its Mississippi data center development even after the state declined to issue a regulatory ruling on the project. The move suggests the developer is willing to absorb regulatory uncertainty to maintain timeline. Mississippi sits within MISO South, where interconnection queues and load growth planning are increasingly stressed.

The Angle Proceeding without a state regulatory ruling creates title and financing risk — lenders and offtake counterparties should pressure-test whether utility service agreements and local permits are fully secured before committing capital.
MISO Christian County, IL · Demand Signal

Eagle Rock Proposes 1GW Illinois Data Center, Start Date 2029–2030

Eagle Rock has proposed a 1GW data center development in Christian County, Illinois, though construction is not expected to begin until 2029–2030. The project would represent a significant load addition to MISO's Illinois footprint, an area already managing coal retirements and renewable buildout simultaneously. The long lead time suggests queue positioning and transmission studies are still in early stages.

The Angle A 2029–2030 start means this is a planning-horizon signal, not near-term revenue — useful for shaping long-dated PPA and transmission capacity conversations, but too early to underwrite merchant generation against.
SPP Topeka, KS · Demand Signal

Evergy Explores Utility-Led Data Center Siting in Topeka

Evergy, the incumbent utility serving Kansas and Missouri, is actively exploring siting a data center in Topeka, Kansas. Utility-led development signals a shift toward vertically integrated load attraction strategies, where the utility controls site, power, and customer relationships. This trend, if successful, could reshape how developers compete for prime Kansas locations.

The Angle Utility-sponsored data centers can crowd out independent developers by offering pre-permitted, rate-advantaged service — monitor Evergy's IRP filings and SPP queue activity in Topeka for signs of reserved capacity that limits third-party access.
ERCOT Waco, TX · Demand Signal

Cipher Mining Targets 500MW Campus Near Retired Waco Coal Plant

Cipher Mining plans to develop a 500MW data center campus near a retired coal plant outside Waco, Texas, with the intent to leverage existing grid infrastructure including transmission lines and substation assets. Brownfield coal sites offer expedited interconnection timelines by utilizing existing high-voltage infrastructure. The Waco area sits within ERCOT's load-heavy North zone.

The Angle Retired coal plant conversions are among the fastest paths to large-load interconnection in ERCOT right now — if Cipher has secured site control and utility coordination, this is a credible near-term load addition that will tighten North zone capacity margins.
ERCOT Texas (Multi-site) · M&A

Hut 8 Prices $4.25B Note to Fund 352MW Hyperscaler-Leased TX Campus

Hut 8 has priced a $4.25 billion note offering to finance a 352MW Texas data center campus that has been pre-leased to an undisclosed hyperscaler. The financing structure and lease pre-commitment signal strong institutional confidence in Texas data center fundamentals. The undisclosed hyperscaler tenant adds intrigue but limits counterparty risk assessment for third parties.

The Angle A fully pre-leased 352MW campus backed by $4.25B in debt financing sets a high pricing benchmark for Texas colocation capacity — use this as a comparable when structuring power supply agreements or evaluating land values near ERCOT load pockets.
ERCOT Childress, TX · Demand Signal

IREN Expands Childress Data Center with Low-Water Footprint Focus

IREN is expanding its Childress, Texas data center campus, citing job creation and a deliberately limited water use profile as key project attributes. Childress sits in the ERCOT Northwest zone, an area with growing wind resource availability but limited local load historically. The low-water positioning may be a response to increasing regulatory and community scrutiny of large data center water consumption.

The Angle IREN's water-efficiency messaging in a rural Texas market is strategic — it lowers local opposition risk and may accelerate permitting, making Childress a potentially smoother development path than more congested urban ERCOT nodes.
NYISO Albany Area, NY · Demand Signal

Prime Companies Unveils $2.8B Kenwood Tech and Data Center Campus

Developer Prime Companies has announced a $2.8 billion Kenwood technology campus and data center plan in the Albany, New York area. The Albany region sits within NYISO Zone F, which has historically benefited from relatively affordable hydropower access and state economic development incentives. New York's data center sector has faced mixed regulatory signals at the state level regarding energy use and emissions.

The Angle Albany's proximity to clean hydro supply and Empire State Development incentives makes it attractive, but NYISO interconnection queues and New York's evolving Climate Leadership and Community Protection Act compliance requirements will be key cost drivers to model early.
