Behind-the-Meter Gas and Captive Finance: How Developers Are Routing Around the Queue

A wave of capital raises and site strategies this week signals that the fastest path to data-center power is increasingly one that never touches the interconnection queue at all.

The dominant theme across this week's deal flow is not any single megawatt figure — it is the structural logic driving developers to sidestep interconnection entirely. Cipher Mining's $810M senior secured notes offering to finance its Stingray site in West Texas, Hut 8's $4.25B note offering tied to its Nueces County campus alongside a 352MW hyperscaler lease, and Corscale and Affinius closing $975M in financing from Blue Owl for a fully leased Northern Virginia campus all reflect the same calculus: lenders and hyperscalers will commit at scale only when power delivery is de-risked, and the interconnection queue — with its multi-year timelines and frequent restudies — is the primary risk variable being engineered out of the equation. The capital markets are not rewarding queue positions; they are rewarding certainty of electrons.

The Stark Power acquisition of Sagebrush sites, funded by up to $140M raised for that purpose with terms undisclosed, is the clearest structural expression of this trend. The Sagebrush assets are co-located natural-gas-fired power plants that bypass the interconnection queue entirely, providing behind-the-meter gas generation that delivers load-ready capacity without a single day spent in a generator interconnection study. For power developers and data-center operators watching queue timelines stretch toward five and six years in PJM and MISO, that model is not a niche workaround — it is a replicable template. Applied Digital's signing of a hyperscaler for a 210MW campus, now its fifth AI campus with three leased to the same tenant, similarly reflects a preference for operators who have already solved the power-delivery problem rather than promising to solve it.

The geographic spread of this week's project signals underscores that no single ISO is absorbing the pressure cleanly. PJM is seeing simultaneous proposals in Goochland County from both Tract and the undisclosed Tuckahoe Tech Park developer on a 900-acre site, as well as activity from Fundamental Data in Mason County, West Virginia and Moonshot Infrastructure in Muncy, Pennsylvania. ERCOT is carrying Hut 8's Nueces County campus and Cipher Mining's West Texas Stingray site simultaneously. MISO is processing DC Blox in Indiana while watching the Panattoni stall in Wisconsin. The distribution matters because each of these ISOs is at a different stage of interconnection reform, and developers are not waiting for reform to catch up — they are pricing in delay as a permanent condition and underwriting accordingly.

The community friction signals are worth holding alongside the capital-markets story. DC Blox's Nashville proposal faces a petition exceeding 386,000 signatures and a potential moratorium, while the Kevin O'Leary-backed Wonder Valley project in Beaver County, Utah is advancing despite a local commissioner moratorium. Kenwood Commons expects to file data-center plans with Albany within two months. These are not isolated permitting headaches — they represent a second chokepoint that is independent of interconnection queues and increasingly shapes site selection. For IPP executives and data-center operators, the practical implication is that behind-the-meter generation and captive financing solve the grid-access problem but do not resolve local land-use opposition, which is now emerging as the next underpriced variable in development timelines.

Campus Announcements — Scale & Power Implications

Hut 8 Locks 352MW Hyperscaler Lease at Nueces County Campus
Hut 8's Beacon Point campus in Nueces County, Texas anchors a $4.25B note offering alongside a 352MW hyperscaler lease commitment.

Hut 8's Beacon Point campus in Nueces County, Texas is emerging as one of the most consequential single-site load commitments in ERCOT this cycle. The company has signed a 352MW hyperscaler lease tied to the campus and is backing the development with a $4.25B note offering — a capital structure that signals long-term, contracted load at a scale that will require serious transmission and interconnection coordination with ERCOT.

For grid practitioners, a 352MW contracted load addition in Nueces County — already a region with active coastal wind and growing industrial demand — warrants close attention to local transformer and substation capacity. The note offering scale suggests Hut 8 is financing full build-out rather than a phased approach, compressing the timeline between permitting and energization.

Applied Digital Signs 210MW Hyperscaler for Fifth AI Campus
Applied Digital's North Dakota expansion adds a 210MW hyperscaler lease, with the same tenant now anchoring three of its campuses.

Applied Digital has signed a hyperscaler for a 210MW campus in North Dakota — its fifth AI campus — with the same unnamed tenant now leasing across three of its facilities. The concentration of load from a single hyperscaler tenant across multiple campuses is a notable structural signal: it suggests a preferred-developer relationship that could accelerate future site selection and queue filings.

From an interconnection standpoint, the North Dakota siting places this load in a region where transmission capacity and generator interconnection queues differ materially from the congested PJM and ERCOT corridors. Practitioners should track whether Applied Digital pursues co-located generation or relies on grid supply for these campuses, as the 210MW figure alone is a meaningful addition for any regional balancing authority.

Cipher Mining Seeks $810M to Finance West Texas AI Site
Cipher Mining is pursuing $810M in senior secured notes for its Stingray data center site in West Texas, backed by Amazon and Fluidstack pre-leases.

Cipher Mining's move to raise $810M in senior secured notes for its Stingray site in West Texas — with Amazon and Fluidstack already holding pre-leases — puts another large-scale ERCOT load project on a near-term financing and development track. Pre-leases from a hyperscaler of Amazon's caliber typically accelerate both lender confidence and interconnection queue priority discussions.

West Texas remains one of the more complex siting environments in ERCOT due to transmission congestion between the wind-rich Panhandle and load centers. A new large data center load in that region could actually improve nodal economics for nearby wind generators during off-peak periods, but practitioners will want to assess whether Stingray's interconnection point sits in a constrained zone that could expose the operator to significant load-side congestion costs.

Goochland County, Virginia Draws Two Competing Campus Proposals
Tract and the 900-acre Tuckahoe Tech Park proposal both target Goochland County, Virginia's West Creek corridor in the same week.

Goochland County, Virginia — adjacent to the established West Creek technology corridor — attracted two separate large data center campus proposals in a single week: one from Tract and a 900-acre site filed under the Tuckahoe Tech Park name by an undisclosed developer. The simultaneous filings underscore how rapidly secondary PJM submarkets are absorbing overflow demand from saturated Northern Virginia nodes.

For interconnection planners, two large-footprint proposals in the same county filed within days of each other create immediate queue sequencing questions. Dominion Energy Virginia's transmission infrastructure in the West Creek area has historically served commercial and mixed-use load; practitioners should monitor whether either project triggers a new bulk transmission upgrade study or whether existing substation capacity can absorb initial phases without significant capital additions.

PPA & Financing

Market Pricing Context

The hyperscaler lease-to-debt financing playbook is printing at scale this cycle. Hut 8's Nueces County campus is the clearest expression: a 352 MW hyperscaler lease anchoring a $4.25B note offering, with the contracted revenue stream doing the heavy lifting on credit support. Cipher Mining is running the same structure at its Stingray site in West Texas—$810M in senior secured notes backstopped by Amazon and Fluidstack pre-leases—confirming that investment-grade or near-investment-grade offtake from a hyperscaler is now the functional substitute for a utility PPA when accessing the high-yield debt markets. Applied Digital's pattern is instructive on the equity side: three campuses leased to the same unnamed hyperscaler tenant, with a 210 MW fifth campus now signed, suggesting that repeat-tenant concentration is being accepted by lenders as a credit positive rather than a concentration risk, at least while hyperscaler demand remains supply-constrained. Corscale and Affinius closing $975M from Blue Owl on a fully leased Northern Virginia asset confirms that private credit is pricing stabilized, lease-up data center paper at terms competitive with traditional construction-to-perm bank debt, compressing spreads for sponsors who can deliver pre-leased product.

On the power procurement side, the signals this week reinforce two diverging strategies for developers who cannot wait on interconnection timelines. Stark Power's acquisition of the Sagebrush portfolio—funded by raising up to $140M with terms undisclosed—is premised entirely on behind-the-meter gas generation bypassing interconnection queues, a structure that trades PPA price exposure and fuel basis risk for speed-to-power certainty. That tradeoff is becoming more economically legible as PJM and ERCOT queue timelines extend; developers are effectively pricing queue delay at a cost high enough to justify on-site gas generation at potentially higher all-in $/MWh versus a utility-scale solar or wind PPA. In ERCOT specifically, the concentration of hyperscaler activity in West Texas and Nueces County suggests bilateral PPA pricing in that region is being set by power buyers with very high willingness-to-pay for firm, dispatchable capacity, which will continue to push merchant and contracted power prices in those nodes above statewide averages. The Wonder Valley situation in Utah—advancing despite a local moratorium—and the cluster of PJM-region announcements in Virginia and West Virginia indicate that developers are stress-testing whether power cost advantages in constrained markets outweigh permitting friction, a calculus that will increasingly show up in PPA tenor and price floor negotiations as lenders demand longer contracted positions to underwrite construction risk.
