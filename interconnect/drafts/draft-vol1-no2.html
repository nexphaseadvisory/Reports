Week of June 10, 2026 · Vol. 1, No. 2
NexPhaseAdvisory.net

Capital formation around data center power is accelerating across every axis this week: Stark Power's $140M acquisition of Sagebrush's 5.6GW development portfolio and Hut 8's $4.25B note offering for a pre-leased Texas campus signal that institutional money is now chasing scale at a pace that outstrips permitting and interconnection timelines. Cipher Mining's move to redevelop a retired coal site near Waco underscores a broader playbook of stranded-asset arbitrage, while Compass Datacenters pressing forward in Mississippi without a state regulatory ruling is a reminder that developers are increasingly willing to absorb regulatory ambiguity rather than lose queue position. Evergy's utility-led site exploration in Topeka and Jane Street's move to self-develop infrastructure mark opposite ends of the vertical integration spectrum, but both point to the same underlying constraint: creditworthy load cannot find enough reliable power fast enough through conventional channels. With announcements spanning ERCOT, PJM, SPP, and international markets from Aragon to Adelaide, the geography of the buildout is widening—but the bottlenecks of interconnection, transmission, and thermal generation remain stubbornly local.

This Week's Signals
ERCOT Nueces County, TX · Demand Signal

Beacon Point AI Eyes Massive Data Center in Nueces County

Beacon Point AI has announced plans for a large-scale data center development in Nueces County, Texas. The project adds to an already congested ERCOT interconnection queue along the Gulf Coast. Specific MW capacity and timeline details have not yet been disclosed.

The Angle Nueces County sits in ERCOT's South load zone with proximity to coastal transmission infrastructure, but queue saturation and transmission constraints near Corpus Christi warrant careful interconnection diligence before committing capital.
ERCOT Texas (statewide) · M&A

Stark Power Acquires 5.6GW Sagebrush Data Center Portfolio

Stark Power has acquired Sagebrush's 5.6GW data center development portfolio, raising approximately $140M to fund the transaction. The deal represents one of the larger portfolio acquisitions in the Texas data center development space. The breadth of the portfolio suggests multiple sites across ERCOT's diverse load zones.

The Angle A 5.6GW portfolio acquisition signals strong conviction in ERCOT's long-term load growth story; developers and investors should track which specific sites progress to interconnection study to gauge realistic near-term deliverability.
MISO Mississippi · Regulatory

Compass Datacenters Pushes Forward Despite Mississippi Regulatory Silence

Compass Datacenters is advancing its Mississippi data center project even after the state declined to issue a regulatory ruling on the development. The regulatory ambiguity introduces uncertainty around utility service obligations and cost recovery mechanisms. Mississippi sits within MISO South, a region with growing but constrained transmission capacity.

The Angle Proceeding without regulatory clarity is a meaningful risk flag — without a ruling, utility cost allocation and interconnection priority could be contested, potentially delaying energization and increasing all-in development costs.
MISO Christian County, IL · Demand Signal

Eagle Rock Proposes 1GW Illinois Data Center, Start Date 2029

Eagle Rock has proposed a 1GW data center in Christian County, Illinois, with construction not expected to begin until 2029–2030. The long lead time places this project squarely in MISO's future planning horizon. Central Illinois has seen increased interest due to relatively affordable land and transmission access.

The Angle The 2029–2030 start date means this project is largely a land and queue placeholder today; early-stage power marketers and interconnection advisors should engage now to shape study positioning before the queue hardens.
NYISO Albany area, NY · Demand Signal

$2.8B Kenwood Tech Campus Planned Near Albany

Prime Companies has unveiled a $2.8B Kenwood tech campus and data center plan in the Albany, New York region. The project would add significant load to NYISO Zone F, which has historically had more transmission headroom than downstate zones. New York's evolving data center regulatory environment, including climate law compliance, adds complexity.

The Angle Albany-area projects benefit from cooler climate reducing cooling loads and proximity to hydro resources, but developers must navigate CLCPA compliance requirements and NYISO's evolving Class Year interconnection process carefully.
SPP Topeka, KS · Demand Signal

Evergy Explores Utility-Led Data Center Siting in Topeka

Evergy is actively exploring siting a data center in Topeka, Kansas, representing a notable shift toward utility-led data center development activity in the SPP footprint. Utility-sponsored projects can benefit from streamlined interconnection and integrated resource planning alignment. Kansas has historically been an underserved market for large-scale data center investment.

The Angle Utility-led development reduces interconnection risk and can accelerate permitting timelines — this is a signal that Evergy sees load growth as a near-term earnings driver, making Topeka a credible greenfield location worth underwriting.
ERCOT Waco, TX · Demand Signal

Cipher Mining Targets 500MW Campus at Retired Coal Plant Near Waco

Cipher Mining is planning a 500MW data center campus near a retired coal plant outside Waco, Texas, leveraging existing grid infrastructure at the brownfield site. Retired generation sites often carry legacy transmission interconnection that can significantly reduce interconnection study timelines and costs. The Waco area falls within ERCOT's North load zone.

The Angle Brownfield coal sites are among the most undervalued real estate in power development — existing substation infrastructure and higher interconnection capacity can compress timelines by 12–18 months versus greenfield; this approach deserves close attention as a replicable model.
ERCOT Texas · M&A

Hut 8 Prices $4.25B Note to Fund 352MW Pre-Leased Texas Campus

Hut 8 has priced a $4.25B note offering to finance a 352MW Texas data center that is pre-leased to an undisclosed hyperscaler. The pre-lease structure significantly de-risks the capital raise and signals strong hyperscaler demand for contracted Texas capacity. The financing scale implies a fully funded, near-term construction-ready project.

The Angle Pre-leased hyperscaler offtake at this scale is the gold standard for project financing in ERCOT — the $12M+/MW implied capital intensity reflects how tight quality capacity has become, and competitors should treat this as a market-clearing price signal.
