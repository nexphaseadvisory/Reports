Week of June 10, 2026 · Vol. 1, No. 2
NexPhaseAdvisory.net

This week's deal flow makes one thing structurally clear: capital is no longer waiting on grid certainty before committing to load. Stark Power's acquisition of Sagebrush's 5.6GW development portfolio—funded by a $140M raise—and Hut 8's $4.25B note offering to finance a pre-leased 352MW Texas campus signal that sophisticated buyers are pricing interconnection risk into the stack and moving anyway. Cipher Mining's pivot to a retired coal site near Waco underscores the brownfield grid infrastructure play gaining traction on ERCOT, while Compass Datacenters advancing its Mississippi project without a state regulatory ruling suggests developers are increasingly willing to absorb jurisdictional ambiguity rather than pause. Taken together, the weight of announcements—spanning Texas, Illinois, Spain, Australia, and upstate New York—reflects a market in which development timelines are stretching toward 2029–2030 while financing structures and site strategies are hardening now.

This Week's Signals
ERCOT Nueces County, TX · Demand Signal

Beacon Point AI Eyes Massive Data Center in Nueces County

Beacon Point AI is planning a large-scale data center development in Nueces County, Texas. The project adds to the accelerating pipeline of AI-driven load growth in ERCOT's coastal zone. Specific MW figures have not yet been disclosed.

The Angle Nueces County sits near existing transmission infrastructure and coastal load pockets. Developers and IPPs should watch interconnection queue dynamics in the ERCOT South region as this adds competitive pressure for capacity.
ERCOT Texas (statewide) · M&A

Stark Power Acquires Sagebrush 5.6GW DC Portfolio for ~$140M

Stark Power has acquired Sagebrush's 5.6GW data center development portfolio, raising approximately $140 million to fund the transaction. The deal represents one of the largest single portfolio acquisitions in the data center development space. The portfolio's geographic concentration has not been fully disclosed but is presumed ERCOT-heavy given Sagebrush's prior activity.

The Angle A 5.6GW portfolio changing hands signals institutional conviction in Texas load growth at scale. Counterparties — from generators to transmission developers — should engage Stark Power early to position on offtake and infrastructure deals.
MISO Mississippi · Regulatory

Compass Datacenters Advances Mississippi Campus Despite Regulatory Uncertainty

Compass Datacenters is pushing forward with its Mississippi data center plans even after the state declined to issue a regulatory ruling on the project. The move suggests the developer is willing to absorb regulatory ambiguity to secure a foothold in an emerging market. Mississippi falls within MISO South, a region with growing interest from large load customers.

The Angle Regulatory silence in Mississippi creates real permitting and utility cost-recovery risk. Developers and lenders considering similar projects in MISO South should pressure-test interconnection and rate treatment assumptions before committing capital.
MISO Christian County, IL · Demand Signal

Eagle Rock Proposes 1GW Illinois Data Center, Construction Starts 2029

Eagle Rock has proposed a 1GW data center in Christian County, Illinois, with construction not expected to begin until 2029–2030. The long lead time suggests the project is still in early development and site control phases. Illinois sits within MISO Midwest, where large load interconnection queues are already congested.

The Angle A 2029 construction timeline means this project will compete in MISO's evolving LGIA queue under future tariff rules. Developers and co-located generation players have a narrow window to get ahead of this load in the interconnection queue.
SPP Topeka, KS · Demand Signal

Evergy Explores Utility-Led Data Center Siting in Topeka

Evergy is actively exploring siting a data center in Topeka, Kansas, marking an unusual move into utility-led development activity. The initiative signals that Evergy sees load attraction as a core growth strategy within its SPP territory. Kansas has historically been an underrepresented market for hyperscale and AI data center investment.

The Angle Utility-sponsored site selection can accelerate permitting and grid integration timelines significantly. Developers and power marketers should engage Evergy directly — utility-backed projects often come with favorable rate structures and de-risked interconnection paths.
ERCOT Waco, TX · Demand Signal

Cipher Mining Targets 500MW Campus Near Retired Coal Plant

Cipher Mining plans a 500MW data center campus near a retired coal plant outside Waco, Texas, leveraging existing substation and transmission infrastructure. The brownfield approach is designed to compress interconnection timelines and reduce infrastructure capital costs. The site's legacy grid assets could support faster energization versus greenfield alternatives.

The Angle Brownfield coal retirements remain one of the most viable fast-path interconnection strategies in ERCOT. Developers and co-located generation providers should actively map retired plant sites — this playbook is repeatable and queue-advantaged.
NYISO Albany, NY · Demand Signal

Prime Companies Unveils $2.8B Kenwood Tech Campus Near Albany

Prime Companies has unveiled a $2.8 billion Kenwood tech campus and data center plan in the Albany, New York area. The project would represent a significant new load addition in NYISO Zone F, which has seen limited large-scale data center development to date. Albany's proximity to fiber corridors and state economic development incentives may underpin the site selection.

The Angle NYISO Zone F has constrained transmission capacity and complex interconnection rules — developers entering this market should conduct rigorous deliverability studies early. The $2.8B price tag suggests serious capital backing, making this a credible demand signal for local generators and utilities.
ERCOT Texas · M&A

Hut 8 Prices $4.25B Note to Fund 352MW Pre-Leased Texas Campus

Hut 8 has priced a $4.25 billion note offering to fund a 352MW Texas data center pre-leased to an undisclosed hyperscaler. The deal demonstrates deep capital market appetite for contracted AI infrastructure in ERCOT. The pre-lease structure significantly de-risks revenue, supporting the large debt raise.

The Angle Pre-leased hyperscaler deals at this scale validate ERCOT as the dominant market for next-generation AI load. Power suppliers and infrastructure investors should treat this as a benchmark transaction — expect similar structures to drive queue activity through 2026.
