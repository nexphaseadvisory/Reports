Nuclear and Coal-CCS Signal PJM's Baseload Bet for Data-Center Load

Two greenfield firm-power projects totaling multi-gigawatt scale are advancing in PJM, reflecting developer confidence that dispatchable, interconnection-queue-eligible generation can command premium off-take from hyperscalers.

Elementl Power is developing up to 1,500 MW of SMR nuclear capacity with GE Vernova Hitachi along the Ohio River, roughly 100 miles southeast of Columbus, while TerraSpark Energy is pursuing a 1,600-MW greenfield coal-plus-CCS campus in West Virginia targeting a 2030 startup, backed by $18.5M in DOE funding. Both projects are sited inside PJM, the grid region absorbing the heaviest hyperscale load growth in the country, and both are pursuing firm, dispatchable interconnection positions rather than the variable-resource queue slots that have historically bottlenecked solar and wind developers.

The pairing matters because it reflects a structural shift in how developers are positioning generation assets: around load certainty rather than energy arbitrage. Microsoft's groundbreaking on a 17-building campus in LaPorte, Indiana, Amazon and Google's multibillion-dollar commitments in Missouri, and Meta's 298-MW solar PPA with RWE in ERCOT all reinforce that hyperscale counterparties are actively locking in long-duration supply across every major grid—creating the off-take visibility that makes capital-intensive, long-lead projects like SMR and CCS financeable.

Watch whether either the Elementl or TerraSpark project secures a hyperscale anchor tenant before entering the formal PJM interconnection queue, as that sequencing—load commitment before queue filing—is emerging as the template for de-risking large baseload interconnection studies. The Birmingham zoning reversal by an unnamed AI firm also signals that municipal permitting is becoming a parallel gating factor alongside FERC queue timelines, particularly for campus-scale loads that trigger infrastructure upgrade studies.

Campus Announcements — Scale & Power Implications

Elementl Power Plans 1.5 GW SMR Project on Ohio River
Elementl Power and GE Vernova Hitachi are developing up to 1,500 MW of small modular reactor capacity roughly 100 miles southeast of Columbus, Ohio, in PJM territory.

The project would represent one of the largest SMR commitments announced in PJM, sited along the Ohio River to leverage existing transmission and cooling infrastructure.

TerraSpark's 1.6 GW Coal-CCS Campus Targets 2030 in West Virginia
TerraSpark Energy is developing a 1,600 MW greenfield coal-plus-CCS campus in West Virginia, backed by $18.5 million in DOE funding and targeting a 2030 startup.

The project would add significant dispatchable capacity to PJM from a state with existing coal supply and transmission infrastructure.

Meta Signs 298 MW Solar PPA with RWE in Texas
Meta has contracted 298 MW of solar generation from RWE for a Texas project, adding to both companies' ERCOT renewable portfolios.

The deal marks a direct offtake commitment from a major hyperscaler to a utility-scale solar developer in one of the country's fastest-growing power markets.

Microsoft Breaks Ground on 17-Building Indiana Data Center Campus
Microsoft has broken ground on a 17-building hyperscale data center campus in La Porte, Indiana, adding a major new load center to MISO.

The campus joins a wave of hyperscale announcements in MISO's footprint, including Amazon and Google committing multibillion-dollar investments in Montgomery County, Missouri.

PPA & Financing

Market Pricing Context

Meta's 298 MW solar PPA with RWE in ERCOT is the clearest utility-scale pricing signal this week, representing a hyperscaler locking in long-dated renewable offtake in one of the most liquid merchant markets in North America — a structure that increasingly sets the floor for corporate PPA benchmarks across the region. With hyperscaler groundbreakings from Microsoft in Indiana and multibillion-dollar commitments from Amazon and Google in Missouri, offtake demand is outpacing contracted supply pipelines in MISO, which should sustain PPA price tension through mid-decade. The Elementl/GE Vernova Hitachi SMR project in PJM and TerraSpark's coal-plus-CCS campus in West Virginia both signal developer appetite for long-tenor, capacity-heavy structures where traditional renewable PPAs can't deliver firm power — implying a bifurcating market where dispatchable assets command a meaningful $/MWh premium over variable renewables. For counterparties underwriting new offtake, the operative implication is that firm, 24/7-capable generation is repricing faster than the headline solar PPA comp would suggest.
