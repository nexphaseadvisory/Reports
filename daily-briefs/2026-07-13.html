Issue No. 558 · US Data Center Power
The morning read on power-constrained demand.
No new qualifications today, plus 4 market developments worth tracking.
Bottom Line
The U.S. data center power market is entering a more complex capital and policy environment, with private equity accelerating co-location of generation and compute assets, state-level tax policy tightening operating economics, and novel distributed capacity structures emerging as viable procurement instruments. EQT's acquisition of Copia Power signals that sophisticated capital is now vertically integrating power origination with data center development, compressing the window for independent advisory value unless that expertise is embedded early in deal formation. Behind-the-meter and alternative generation pathways — fuel cells, VPPs — are moving from pilot concepts to structured offtake products, expanding the surface area where interconnection and PPA origination expertise applies. North Carolina's electricity tax repeal is a leading indicator that state-level fiscal pressure on data center power costs will intensify, forcing developers to prioritize advisory rigor around long-term supply structuring. The overall signal is that power origination and interconnection advisory capabilities are becoming a competitive differentiator at the earliest stages of data center capital formation, not an afterthought.

Strategic Thought  Vertical integration of power origination within data center development platforms is redefining where independent advisory value accretes — it must now enter at the acquisition underwriting or greenfield site-selection stage rather than post-development. As more private equity sponsors replicate the Copia model, the market for embedded interconnection and PPA origination advisory at the platform level — rather than the project level — is likely to expand. Advisors with ISO/RTO queue navigation and PPA structuring depth will face both greater demand and earlier competitive displacement if they are not positioned at the capital formation stage.

North Carolina  NORTH CAROLINA
North Carolina repealed its sales tax exemption on electricity consumed by data centers while retaining capital equipment incentives, projected to raise approximately $21.4 million in fiscal years 2026–2027.
Airtable  Open record
Read  The repeal materially raises operating costs for data centers in one of the Southeast's most active development markets, creating direct pressure on operators to reduce power cost exposure through more sophisticated long-term supply structuring. Developers and hyperscalers with North Carolina footprints will face increased urgency around locking in favorable PPA terms or optimizing interconnection positioning to access lower-cost generation zones.
Strategic Thought  State-level fiscal policy targeting data center electricity is emerging as a new cost variable that operators can only partially offset through capital incentives, making power procurement advisory a higher-ROI engagement relative to the cost of the tax burden itself. North Carolina's move may accelerate a pattern in which states with constrained grid infrastructure use tax policy to manage data center load growth, indirectly increasing demand for interconnection advisory that can identify lower-friction, lower-cost-power jurisdictions. The signal reinforces that origination expertise — particularly in identifying PPA structures and grid access points that hedge against operating cost escalation — is gaining strategic weight in site selection decisions.

News — Canary Media
Sunrun, Tesla, and Renew Home announced a partnership to aggregate rooftop solar, home batteries, and smart thermostats into what is described as the largest virtual power plant configuration in the United States, targeting data center load hotspots.
Airtable  Open record
Read  The aggregation of distributed residential assets into grid-scale capacity products represents a structural shift in what qualifies as a viable power supply instrument for large commercial and industrial offtakers, including data center operators. VPP structures introduce new complexity in contract design, capacity assurance, and interconnection coordination that did not exist under conventional utility PPA frameworks.
Strategic Thought  As VPP aggregation matures into a recognized capacity product, data center operators and developers will require advisory expertise capable of evaluating distributed supply structures against conventional generation PPAs on a like-for-like basis — a capability gap that is not currently well-served by traditional energy counsel or real estate-oriented data center advisors. The intersection of VPP aggregation and ISO/RTO interconnection rules creates a specialized structuring challenge, particularly in markets where demand response and distributed capacity resources must be registered, stacked, and dispatched under complex tariff frameworks. Origination advisors with ISO/RTO market fluency are better positioned than generalist consultants to evaluate whether VPP-backed supply arrangements can reliably meet data center power quality and availability requirements.

Siemens, FuelCell Energy
Siemens and FuelCell Energy announced a partnership to deliver fuel cell-based power solutions targeting data centers and other large industrial energy consumers.
Airtable  Open record
Read  The partnership signals that behind-the-meter generation alternatives are being purpose-engineered and commercially packaged for data center offtakers at scale, moving fuel cells from niche demonstration projects to structured procurement options. For data center developers evaluating power supply strategies, fuel cell products now represent a credible alternative or complement to grid interconnection — particularly in constrained queue markets.
Strategic Thought  The emergence of purpose-built, vendor-backed behind-the-meter generation products for data centers creates a new category of power procurement decision that sits at the boundary of interconnection strategy and alternative generation origination, requiring advisors who can evaluate grid-connected versus islanded or hybrid supply configurations on technical and commercial terms simultaneously. In markets where ISO/RTO interconnection queues are severely congested — MISO, PJM, CAISO — behind-the-meter fuel cell products may reduce or defer grid interconnection requirements, altering the value calculus for queue position and substation capacity reservation. Advisors capable of modeling the tradeoffs between queue-dependent grid supply and fuel cell-backed behind-the-meter solutions will have a differentiated position as data center developers face binary capacity decisions under compressed timelines.
