Bottom Line
The data center power-origination market is operating at peak velocity across every major ISO/RTO, with capital commitments measured in billions closing on near-weekly cadences and grid interconnection queues — most visibly ERCOT's newly launched Batch Zero process for a 438 GW backlog — reshaping how developers must sequence site selection, power procurement, and financing. Hyperscaler lease signings at Applied Digital, Fleet, and Corscale signal that tenants are committing before power is fully secured, compressing the window for developers to execute interconnection and PPA strategy. Simultaneously, TVA's draft IRP projecting up to 26 GW of new gas reflects a regional load-growth reality that is outpacing even utility planning assumptions, while ERCOT's structural rule change institutionalizes a new competitive discipline around large-load queue positioning. Capital markets remain highly receptive — nine-figure senior secured financings, Blue Owl refinancings, and SPAC combinations are all closing — but each close accelerates the clock on power decisions that must follow. Developers who have freshly closed financing or signed leases without an executed power strategy represent the highest-urgency advisory opportunity in the current market.

The commissioning also validates Kern County's transmission infrastructure as capable of absorbing large-scale storage interconnection, which has positive implications for subsequent queue positioning by other developers seeking to pair storage with solar or wind in the same substation vicinity.
