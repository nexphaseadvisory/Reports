Bottom Line
The data center power market is at an inflection point, with ERCOT's formal launch of Batch Zero large-load interconnection studies for a 438 GW queue — roughly 90% data center driven — signaling that grid access is now a gating constraint rather than a background task. Concurrent capital events across the qualified lead universe, including DataBank's $650M Red Oak upsize, Applied Digital's dual hyperscaler lease signings, Corscale's $975M Blue Owl refinancing, and Fleet's $4.6B notes close, confirm that deployment capital is committing faster than interconnection and power-origination infrastructure can absorb it. Virginia's first-ever data center electricity tax on self-generated power and Baltimore's development moratorium are reshaping Mid-Atlantic siting economics in real time, redirecting demand pressure into adjacent PJM nodes and intensifying competition for queue position. TVA's draft IRP contemplating 7–26 GW of new gas signals that even historically stable load-serving territories are being forced to replan capacity at unprecedented scale. Across every major ISO — PJM, ERCOT, MISO, WECC — the window between capital commitment and power delivery is compressing, elevating the strategic value of early interconnection positioning and structured PPA origination.
