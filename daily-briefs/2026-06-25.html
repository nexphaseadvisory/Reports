Bottom Line
The U.S. data center power market is at a structural inflection point: ERCOT's Batch Zero large-load interconnection process has launched against a 438 GW queue that is approximately 90% data center demand, while PJM/Dominion territory faces simultaneous pressure from Northern Virginia hyperscale build-out and a new Virginia self-generation power tax that reshapes behind-the-meter economics. Capital commitment velocity across the qualified pipeline — exceeding $15B in new financing and lease signings within the last 90 days — is outpacing interconnection queue capacity across every major U.S. ISO/RTO. Developers and owner-operators with committed capital but no executed power strategy are the defining profile of this market moment, and the window to secure favorable queue positions ahead of ERCOT's August 2026 batch study cycle and PJM's next cluster study round is closing fast. The gap between policy reform progress and actual interconnection processing speed, documented by Advanced Energy United, confirms that expert queue navigation remains a scarce and high-value capability regardless of regulatory progress.
