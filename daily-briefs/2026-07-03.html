Bottom Line
PJM's July 2026 backstop procurement filing and FERC's simultaneous show-cause orders to all major U.S. grid operators mark a structural inflection point: regulatory frameworks governing large-load interconnection are being rewritten in real time, compressing the window for developers and data center buyers to lock in queue position and capacity contracts. Meanwhile, site-control activity — from Michigan campus expansions to Virginia parcel assembly near AWS infrastructure — signals that developer capital is deploying faster than power access can be secured. The Eagan Capital moratorium lawsuit and Karis Cold's Hoffman Estates rezoning withdrawal both illustrate how regulatory friction is forcing project pivots that require immediate interconnection strategy reassessment. Across MISO, PJM, and WECC, the gap between announced megawatt demand and actionable queue-ready capacity continues to widen.

PJM Interconnection
PJM's board is preparing a backstop capacity procurement filing with FERC in July 2026, targeting a September auction to address near-term supply shortfalls driven by accelerating data center load growth.
Read  A September backstop auction creates a compressed, high-stakes window for capacity resources to establish qualification and market position before the clearing event. Developers and data center buyers without finalized interconnection or offtake arrangements face material risk of being priced out or excluded from the auction mechanism entirely.
Strategic Thought  The backstop auction effectively establishes a hard deadline for interconnection readiness in PJM, converting what had been a multi-year queue optimization exercise into a near-term positioning sprint. Power originators and interconnection advisors with active PJM queue relationships are positioned to deliver disproportionate value in the weeks between the FERC filing and the September clearing date. For data center buyers relying on merchant capacity, the auction outcome will set a pricing floor that makes bilateral PPA origination — completed before the clearing event — significantly more attractive on a cost basis.
