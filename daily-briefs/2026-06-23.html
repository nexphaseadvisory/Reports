Bottom Line
Today's signals confirm that the AI-driven data center power surge has moved well past the announcement phase and into the messy reality of interconnection queues, community opposition, regulatory intervention, and capital deployment at scale. FERC's large-load directive and ERCOT's new interconnection framework are structural inflection points — RTOs are no longer passively absorbing queue volume, they're actively reshaping the rules, and anyone without a sophisticated queue strategy is already behind. The geographic spread is striking: Kansas, Wyoming, Wisconsin, West Virginia, Virginia, and Texas are all lighting up simultaneously, which means SPP, ATC, PJM, and ERCOT are all under simultaneous pressure. The technology mix is also diversifying fast — geothermal, offshore wind, solar-plus-storage, and behind-the-meter gas are all being evaluated as data center fuel sources, signaling that no single generation thesis is winning. The window to be the expert in the room for UNKNOWN-sophistication developers at GW-scale campuses is open right now, before they build an in-house bench.
