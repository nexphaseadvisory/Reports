Bottom Line
The U.S. data center and energy infrastructure market is accelerating on multiple fronts simultaneously, with hyperscaler-anchored mega-projects, multi-billion-dollar asset recapitalizations, and first-time entrants all converging on the same bottleneck: grid interconnection and structured power offtake. Cypress Creek's 2.5 GW Steel River groundbreak and Stonebridge's $15B Pennsylvania campus proposal in a single news cycle underscore that the demand for PPA structuring and interconnection expertise is no longer episodic — it is the central critical path for capital deployment at scale. Bitcoin miners are pivoting aggressively into power-site acquisition, with Mara's July 2 closing of the Volt Texas/MAT 1177 transaction confirming that digital-asset operators now compete directly with developers for interconnection-ready land. Across segments — hyperscale, colocation, renewables development, and crypto mining — the constraint is identical: power position, not capital. Advisory professionals with FERC, ISO/RTO interconnection, and PPA origination depth occupy the scarcest and most leverageable seat at the table.

Strategic Thought  The Steel River transaction illustrates that interconnection-ready, utility-scale solar-plus-storage projects with pre-contracted hyperscaler offtake are now the benchmark deal structure in U.S. renewable energy development. Advisors with simultaneous depth in FERC interconnection queue management, PPA origination, and storage integration are positioned at the exact nexus this deal type requires. As hyperscalers accelerate 24/7 carbon-free energy commitments, the pipeline of similarly structured mega-projects is likely to grow, expanding demand for origination expertise beyond the developer community to include the off-takers themselves seeking advisory support on deal terms and grid access.

Stonebridge Associates  PENNSYLVANIA
Stonebridge Associates has proposed a 16-building, $15 billion data center campus on a 569-acre former dynamite factory site in Lawrence County, Pennsylvania, to be served by Penn Power.
Airtable  Open record
Read  A first-time data center entrant proposing a $15 billion campus on a brownfield site served by a single distribution utility faces the full sequence of interconnection complexity: site-specific substation design, transmission upgrade studies, PJM queue entry, and utility coordination with Penn Power — none of which is routine for a developer without existing power infrastructure experience. The scale and novelty of the proponent make this one of the clearest near-term openings for interconnection advisory engagement in the current signal set.
Strategic Thought  Brownfield data center proposals of this magnitude in PJM territory are multiplying as developers seek sites with existing industrial infrastructure and potential grid proximity, but the interconnection pathway for a campus requiring hundreds of megawatts from a regional distribution utility is materially more complex than the site's prior industrial use implies. PJM's cluster study process, combined with Penn Power's distribution-level constraints, means the project's power timeline is the most uncertain element of its development schedule — and the element most likely to determine whether the $15 billion investment thesis holds. For the broader interconnection advisory market, proposals like Stonebridge's represent a category of client that is capital-rich and technically underequipped on power, the profile that generates the highest advisory leverage.

While European grid operators operate under different regulatory regimes than FERC-jurisdictional U.S. ISOs, the structural advisory gap — capital sophistication without power-origination depth — is identical to the pattern emerging in U.S. markets, and signals that demand for this advisory profile is becoming a global rather than domestic market dynamic.
