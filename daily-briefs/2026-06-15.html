4 developments worth a power developer’s attention today, ranked by where an independent originator can actually create value.
Bottom Line
Today's signals converge on a single theme: capital is moving fast and at scale into AI compute infrastructure, and the power procurement race is intensifying across geographies — Wyoming, northern France, and western China all in one day. The Prometheus and Data4 announcements confirm that sophisticated hyperscalers are not waiting on grid readiness; they are betting on brownfield sites and emerging markets precisely because transmission is constrained everywhere else. The Jinko Power solar-to-DC integration model in western China is the behind-the-meter playbook being stress-tested at gigawatt scale — that template will arrive in US markets sooner than most expect. The Tapestry-PJM signal is the sleeper here: if AI can compress interconnection application processing from weeks to under an hour, queue throughput in PJM could accelerate materially, which reshapes siting economics for both generation and load. The center of gravity today is not any single deal — it is the structural acceleration of power demand meeting a market where interconnection tooling is finally trying to catch up.

Prometheus Hyperscale  WYOMING  ACTIONABILITY 4 
Prometheus Hyperscale is advancing a $1.2 billion data center campus in Cheyenne, Wyoming, requiring firm power and large-scale interconnection structuring in a WECC market with constrained transmission headroom.
Read  A hyperscale commitment of this magnitude in Wyoming signals that sophisticated developers are willing to absorb WECC interconnection complexity and PacifiCorp load queue risk in exchange for land availability and relatively lower power costs — a revealing bet on where the supply-demand curve is heading.
The Angle  This tells me that even in markets with real transmission friction, well-capitalized hyperscalers with in-house energy teams are clearing their own path rather than waiting for grid buildout. The Wyoming move specifically suggests Prometheus sees a near-term window in PacifiCorp territory before that queue hardens the way CAISO and PJM already have. I want to track this campus as a leading indicator — if they are moving $1.2B into Cheyenne, the WECC load interconnection queue is about to get measurably more crowded, which affects every generation developer I know working in that footprint. That is the market signal worth internalizing right now.
2
Data4  ACTIONABILITY 3 
Data4, owned by Brookfield and operating campuses across six European countries, has confirmed a €5 billion, 700MW AI data center campus on a former steelworks brownfield site in northern France.
Read  The brownfield steelworks selection is a deliberate workaround for greenfield grid connection timelines in France — existing high-voltage infrastructure on industrial sites is becoming a structural procurement advantage in European markets where RTE interconnection queues are measured in years.
The Angle  This is a clear market signal that brownfield-to-hyperscale conversion is becoming a repeatable playbook in Europe, driven by the same transmission scarcity dynamics I see daily in US markets. The steelworks site almost certainly comes with legacy HV substation infrastructure that compresses interconnection lead time — that is the core asset, not the land itself. For my US origination work, this reinforces that load-side developers who can identify and control brownfield industrial sites near existing transmission are building a durable competitive moat. I am watching whether this model accelerates in US industrial belt markets like PJM-West and MISO-Midwest next.
3
Jinko Power  ACTIONABILITY 2 
Jinko Power is planning a 1GW solar-powered AI data center in western China, co-locating utility-scale generation directly with hyperscale compute load in a behind-the-meter integration model.
Read  A 1GW behind-the-meter solar-plus-DC build at this scale functions as a live proof-of-concept for the vertically integrated power-to-compute model that US developers have been discussing theoretically — the technical and commercial feasibility questions are being answered in real time, just in a different jurisdiction.
The Angle  I keep watching Jinko's moves because they are a sophisticated developer stress-testing the exact structure that US hyperscalers are circling — vertically integrated renewable generation locked to a captive AI load, eliminating merchant risk on both sides of the meter. The western China geography is chosen for the same reason Wyoming and northern France are being targeted: abundant land, lower grid congestion, and access to cheap renewable resource. When this model migrates to US markets — and it will — the developers who move first on combined-use interconnection applications and co-located load agreements in ERCOT and WECC will have a years-long head start. This is the structural template I am tracking most closely heading into 2026.
4
Tapestry  PENNSYLVANIA  ACTIONABILITY 1 
Google-backed Tapestry has completed its first live deployment of an AI platform for PJM, processing 811 generation interconnection applications in under an hour — a task that previously consumed weeks of manual queue management work.
Read  If AI-driven queue processing compresses PJM application throughput at this rate, the bottleneck in PJM interconnection may shift from administrative processing to physical network studies and transmission upgrade timelines, which could alter where and how fast generation capacity can realistically reach NTP.
The Angle  This is genuinely interesting to me as someone who has run PJM interconnection processes the hard way — 811 applications processed in under an hour is a step-function change in administrative throughput, and I want to understand exactly where in the FERC Order 2023 workflow this sits. What I do not yet know is whether Tapestry is positioning as pure infrastructure tooling or whether they are building toward commercial origination services that would put them in adjacent territory to what I do. Before I form a stronger view, I would want a direct conversation with their team to understand the roadmap — a short DM to their leadership costs nothing and could surface a partnership angle or at minimum sharpen my read on where PJM queue velocity is heading in the next 12 months.
What I’d Act On
Tapestry is the one to engage today — the PJM deployment is live, the Google backing gives it institutional credibility, and I have no read yet on whether they are looking for domain partners who can translate queue acceleration into commercial origination strategy; a quick DM to their leadership to understand the roadmap is low-cost and high-optionality. The Prometheus Cheyenne campus is also worth a market-intelligence touchpoint with counterparts there — not to pitch, but because a $1.2B Wyoming build at that scale will surface real data on WECC transmission constraints and PacifiCorp load interconnection timelines that would sharpen my own market view.
