Bottom Line
The data center power market is experiencing a structural acceleration in which capital commitment, site control, and interconnection queue positioning are all converging simultaneously rather than sequentially, compressing the advisory window for any given project. PJM and ERCOT are projected to have no spare capacity beyond 2027 against a 39% demand growth backdrop, meaning developers who have not yet locked interconnection strategy are already behind the curve. A $4.75B Alphabet-Intersect PPA, a $650M DataBank construction financing upsize, a $975M Corscale refinancing, and a $5.2B Applied Digital lease—all within the past 17 days—confirm that hyperscale capital is deploying at a pace that outstrips grid readiness in every major ISO territory. Regulatory and community resistance, exemplified by Thor Equities' federal lawsuit and Sentinel DC's Maine withdrawal, is reshaping where and how developers commit capital, driving a flight toward sites with cleaner permitting profiles and pre-secured grid access. The combined pressure of capacity scarcity, accelerating capital deployment, and rising project-level political risk creates sustained demand for specialized interconnection and power-origination advisory across the pipeline.
