Bottom Line
The data center power-procurement market is operating at peak urgency across multiple ISOs simultaneously, with new campus groundbreakings, freshly approved municipal projects, and multi-hundred-megawatt interconnection filings all landing within the same 30-day window. FERC's directive to RTOs to expedite large-load interconnection processes is reshaping the competitive landscape in real time, compressing the window developers have to secure queue position before new rules take effect. Capital is clearly not the binding constraint — the $975M Blue Owl refinancing at Corscale, the $400M+ Galaxy Digital campus approval, and the $4.75B Alphabet-Intersect PPA collectively confirm that institutional money is chasing power-ready sites, not the reverse. The binding constraint is interconnection expertise and origination strategy, with PJM and ERCOT both projected to exhaust spare capacity by 2027 per ICF modeling. Developers and owner-operators who have not yet secured credible grid-access strategies face a rapidly closing window before queue congestion and regulatory change lock in disadvantaged positions.
