Bottom Line
Today's signals converge on a clear theme: capital is moving aggressively into greenfield and brownfield large-scale AI data center campuses, and the power procurement challenge is the long pole in the tent for both. Wyoming and northern France represent different regulatory and grid realities, but both share the same structural bottleneck — firm, deliverable power at scale on a timeline that matches construction ambition. The DEVELOPING profile at Prometheus signals an organization that has power ambition but is still assembling the internal muscle to execute it, making this a warm origination conversation. Data4's SOPHISTICATED posture and Brookfield ownership tells a more important story about where institutional capital is placing its next big bet in European AI infrastructure.

Prometheus Hyperscale  WYOMING  ACTIONABILITY 4 
Prometheus Hyperscale is advancing a $1.2B data center campus in Cheyenne, Wyoming, requiring firm power procurement and interconnection structuring within the WECC/PACE footprint.
Read  A campus of this scale in Wyoming will require meaningful engagement with Rocky Mountain Power and potentially PACE transmission queues, and a developer without deep in-house origination capability will hit friction early on resource adequacy, firm energy sourcing, and LGIA negotiation.
The Angle  Before I pick up the phone, I want to confirm who is actually running their power workstream — do they have an energy lead on staff or is this being handled by their development team ad hoc? If it's the latter, that's my entry point. I've done this exact work in WECC, and the Wyoming interconnection and PPA landscape has nuances that trip up even experienced teams. My approach would be a low-pressure technical conversation framed around what they've already encountered in the interconnection queue, not a pitch — let the complexity of the problem open the door.
2
Data4  ACTIONABILITY 3 
Data4, backed by Brookfield Asset Management, has confirmed a €5 billion, 700MW AI data center development on a brownfield steelworks site in northern France, representing one of the largest single-site European AI infrastructure commitments to date.
Read  A 700MW brownfield interconnection in France signals that institutional capital has concluded European grid access constraints are solvable at this scale — and that RTE's transmission infrastructure, combined with a former industrial site's existing grid footprint, is being treated as a genuine fast-path to energization.
The Angle  This isn't a company I'm calling to offer help — Data4 and Brookfield have the origination and procurement machinery to run this themselves. What this signal tells me is that the brownfield industrial site playbook is gaining serious institutional validation in Europe, and I should be watching whether that thesis migrates back to North America. There are former industrial corridors in PJM and MISO with similar grid-adjacent characteristics, and if Brookfield is making this bet at 700MW in France, it's worth stress-testing whether that same capital logic is informing their North American pipeline.
What I’d Act On
Prometheus Hyperscale is the priority call today — a $1.2B campus in Wyoming with DEVELOPING sophistication means they have power exposure they know about but may lack the WECC/PACE interconnection depth to fully navigate. Data4 is worth a watch, not a pitch — Brookfield's infrastructure apparatus has real origination capability, but the 700MW brownfield RTE interconnection play in France is a market signal worth tracking for what it tells you about European grid appetite for AI load.
