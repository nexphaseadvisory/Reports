Bottom Line
Today's signal cluster is unmistakably centered on two converging forces: hyperscaler power demand pulling forward large-scale solar and LDES origination, and a technology diversification wave in storage — CO2 batteries, VRFB, sodium-ion — that is creating novel interconnection and capacity-accreditation complexity that most developers are not yet equipped to navigate. The ITC safe harbor deadline is compressing decision timelines across the solar stack, forcing developers into interconnection and procurement sequencing choices that carry real basis risk. Meanwhile, the Northeast offshore HVDC push and MISO/ERCOT activity confirm that interconnection queue positioning is the critical bottleneck asset in every major ISO right now. Capital is flowing — $3.5B to Cypress Creek, $1.2B to Prometheus, €5B to Data4 — but the constraint is not money, it is grid access and offtake certainty. The firms that can structure firm interconnection positions and creditworthy offtake simultaneously are the ones capturing NTP value.
