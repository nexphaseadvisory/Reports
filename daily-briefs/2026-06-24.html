Issue No. 539 · US Data Center Power
The morning read on power-constrained demand.
No qualified leads today — watch list and market context below, plus 39 developments worth a power developer’s attention.
Bottom Line
Today's signals converge on a single structural reality: the U.S. grid is being overwhelmed by data center load at every level simultaneously — ERCOT's 438 GW queue is now 90% data centers and just launched Batch Zero, FERC is warning RTOs to self-correct on large-load interconnection or face agency-mandated solutions, and TVA is contemplating 7–26 GW of new gas just to keep pace with load growth that is already outrunning its IRP reference case. The supply side is responding — record Q1 2026 storage deployments, a freshly operational 400 MW Utah solar-plus-storage project, and California's first 8-hour BESS commissioning — but generation and interconnection pipelines remain structurally mismatched to the pace and geography of load growth. Regulatory friction is intensifying in parallel: Virginia's new data center power tax reshapes BTM economics, Oracle is suing Wisconsin regulators over tariff credits, and community opposition is stalling projects from Scotland to western Washington to Nashville. The net read for a power origination professional is that queue strategy, substation siting, and PPA structuring expertise have rarely been more consequential — and the window to position ahead of tightening RTO frameworks is compressing fast.
