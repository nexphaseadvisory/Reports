Bottom Line
The U.S. large-load interconnection market reached an inflection point this week, with ERCOT's 438 GW queue prompting a formal Batch Zero process and FERC issuing a direct warning to RTOs that agency-mandated solutions will follow if large-load frameworks are not self-corrected. TVA's draft IRP now contemplates 7–26 GW of new gas generation as load growth in its territory already exceeds reference-case forecasts, reinforcing that the Southeast is entering a sustained capacity-deficit cycle. Kansas is emerging as a new focal point for gigawatt-scale data center development, with at least two independent proposals (Digital Realty and Diode Ventures) surfacing in SPP/Evergy territory within the same news cycle. Record Q1 2026 U.S. energy storage installations and the commissioning of California's first 8-hour BESS signal that capital is deploying aggressively into storage, but interconnection queue congestion and community opposition are increasingly the binding constraints on project timelines. The convergence of regulatory escalation, queue saturation, and accelerating load growth is compressing the window for developers and owner-operators to establish defensible interconnection positions before framework changes impose new costs and timelines.
