Bottom Line
Today's signals converge on a single structural theme: interconnection capacity is becoming the binding constraint on AI infrastructure buildout, and regulators are moving to codify that reality. FERC's large-load directive and ERCOT's Batch Zero launch are not incremental policy tweaks — they are market structure resets that will sort winners from losers in the queue over the next 18 months. Capital is flowing at unprecedented scale into generation, storage, and data center assets simultaneously, but the bottleneck is grid access, not capital or compute. The geographic spread — Wyoming, Kansas, Wisconsin, West Virginia, Texas, California — confirms this is a national phenomenon with no single RTO absorbing all the pressure. Developers and buyers who move now on queue positioning and PPA structuring before new RTO frameworks lock in will have a durable advantage over those who wait for rules to fully settle.
