Bottom Line
The data center power market continues to bifurcate between scaled hyperscaler-anchored platforms locking in long-term capacity and smaller or first-time developers getting caught in entitlement and interconnection bottlenecks. Hut 8's second 15-year, 704MW fully commercialized Texas campus signals that power-first platforms with clean interconnection queues command premium hyperscaler demand, while Wildflower's dual-moratorium stall on Long Island illustrates that CRE-heritage developers remain structurally exposed when regulatory and power complexity intersects. Hedgehog USA's Idemitsu supply agreement and Whiterose's ComEd filing in a constrained Chicago market both reflect a cohort of developing-sophistication operators executing real capital commits without in-house power origination depth. The market signal across all four names is consistent: interconnection queue positioning and PPA structuring expertise are rate-limiting variables, not capital availability.
