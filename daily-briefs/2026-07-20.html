Bottom Line
The large-load data center power market is entering a phase of simultaneous regulatory, siting, and interconnection stress across multiple ISOs and non-ISO utility territories, with new tariff frameworks emerging in the West and community opposition intensifying in the Pacific Northwest and Midwest. Developers operating in ERCOT face compounding execution pressure as multi-gigawatt campus pipelines advance faster than internal power teams can manage. Non-ISO utility jurisdictions — including Alabama Power and Oncor service territories — are presenting structurally opaque interconnection pathways that require specialized negotiation capability beyond what most lean developer teams carry in-house. Watch list companies with unresolved power procurement gaps in ERCOT, PJM, MISO, and SERC remain the highest-priority engagement targets in the current cycle. The regulatory calendar is accelerating, with tariff working groups, zoning decisions, and town hall proceedings each capable of triggering immediate advisory demand.
