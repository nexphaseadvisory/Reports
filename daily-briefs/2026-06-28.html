Bottom Line
The data center power procurement market reached a new urgency threshold this week, with Galaxy Digital's 500-acre, $400M+ Project Merlin receiving formal municipal approval in ERCOT territory and Edged topping out its 200MW Council Bluffs campus — two discrete signals that large-scale developers are moving from planning into execution phases where interconnection queue positioning and PPA structuring become rate-limiting constraints. Concurrently, FERC's regulatory push to accelerate large-load grid connections is reshaping the queue navigation environment, creating both new pathways and new complexity for developers without in-house ISO/RTO expertise. Colorado community moratoriums and Virginia's emerging 900MW campus proposal illustrate that the supply-demand imbalance in power-constrained markets is intensifying pressure on developers to demonstrate credible grid access strategies before municipal and regulatory bodies. The aggregate picture is one of a market where execution-stage power advisory capability — spanning interconnection queue management, PPA origination, and regulatory navigation — is becoming a determinative factor in whether large-scale campus projects advance or stall.

Undisclosed  VIRGINIA
Goochland County, Virginia is weighing a proposed technology overlay zone that would enable a 900MW data center campus, with Denver-based developer Tract identified as the project proponent.
Read  A 900MW campus in Virginia's data center alley represents one of the largest single-site power commitments currently in public view, and the overlay zone application signals the project is in early entitlement — meaning interconnection queue filing and Dominion Energy Virginia PPA structuring decisions are imminent. Tract's brand positioning around 'pre-positioned power' suggests the firm has some internal grid strategy capability, though the scale of a 900MW Dominion interconnection request in a congested PJM subregion would stress any in-house team.
Strategic Thought  Projects of this scale in PJM/Dominion territory are navigating one of the most backlogged interconnection queues in North America, where 900MW of new load requires careful transmission cost allocation analysis, cluster study positioning, and potentially behind-the-meter or co-located generation structuring to achieve commercially viable delivery timelines. The technology overlay zone pathway — while expediting local land use — does nothing to accelerate FERC-jurisdictional interconnection processes, meaning the gap between entitlement speed and grid access speed is widening for developers pursuing this site type. For the interconnection advisory market, large Virginia campuses of this size are becoming a defining test case for whether pre-positioned power claims can survive the rigor of PJM's CIFP cluster process.

Cummins  TEXAS
A Texas data center project is deploying Cummins natural gas generators for backup power, highlighting on-site generation as a primary resilience strategy in ERCOT territory.
Read  Natural gas backup generation at scale in ERCOT is a well-established resilience architecture, but the choice signals that the project either lacks firm grid capacity commitments or is structuring around ERCOT's energy-only market design where on-site generation provides both backup and potential economic dispatch optionality. Cummins as the equipment provider — rather than as a project developer — limits the direct advisory relevance to the data center operator.
Strategic Thought  The broader market signal is that ERCOT's lack of a capacity market continues to drive data center developers toward on-site generation investments as a substitute for grid reliability guarantees, creating a parallel advisory market around hybrid grid-plus-generation structuring that requires both interconnection queue expertise and gas supply or PPA origination capability. As natural gas generation at data centers scales, the regulatory and permitting complexity — including air permits, fuel supply agreements, and ERCOT large-load registration — increases proportionally, reinforcing demand for integrated power advisory that spans both the utility interconnection and behind-the-meter generation tracks.
4
FERC
FERC has issued an order directing faster grid interconnection access for AI data centers, representing a regulatory effort to create accelerated large-load connection pathways.
Read  FERC's intervention signals that the agency views interconnection queue backlog as a material constraint on AI infrastructure deployment — a posture that could produce new procedural rules, expedited study timelines, or dedicated large-load queue tracks across PJM, MISO, CAISO, and SPP. The order does not eliminate queue complexity but changes the regulatory environment in which developers and advisors navigate it.
Strategic Thought  Regulatory acceleration of large-load interconnection, if implemented through new FERC rulemaking, would compress the window between queue application and commercial operation — simultaneously raising the value of advisors who can execute quickly within new procedural frameworks and lowering barriers for developers who previously faced multi-year study timelines. The transition period between the order and implemented rules is typically where queue positioning expertise is most valuable, as early movers who understand the new process design can secure advantaged positions before the broader market adapts. For the interconnection advisory market, FERC's posture reinforces that large-load queue navigation is a specialized discipline attracting both regulatory and commercial attention at the highest levels.
5
Multiple Colorado communities  COLORADO
Multiple Colorado municipalities have imposed moratoriums on new data center development, citing concerns over power consumption, water use, and tax incentive structures.
Read  Community-level moratoriums in Colorado create a bifurcated development environment where projects with credible, publicly defensible power and resource plans are better positioned to survive council review while underprepared proposals face indefinite delays or outright rejection. The moratoriums reflect a broader national pattern of local governments demanding substantive grid impact and resource disclosure before approving large loads.
Strategic Thought  For the interconnection advisory market, municipal moratoriums signal that grid impact analysis and community power narrative — historically back-office functions — are becoming front-end entitlement requirements that can determine whether a project advances to interconnection application at all. Developers operating in moratorium-prone markets increasingly need integrated advisory that connects ISO/RTO queue strategy to the public-facing grid impact disclosures that local governments are demanding, creating a new layer of advisory value upstream of the formal interconnection process. Colorado's WECC/Western interconnect context also means that transmission constraint and resource adequacy arguments are technically complex, further differentiating advisors with deep ISO-level modeling capability from generalist consultants.
