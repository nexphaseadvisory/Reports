Bottom Line
The U.S. data center power market is operating in a compressed-capital, high-velocity mode: multiple gigawatt-scale lease signings, nine-figure financings, and new interconnection filings have stacked within a 17-day window ending June 25, 2026, signaling that power origination and interconnection advisory demand is at a cyclical peak. Project withdrawals — such as Sentinel DC's exit from Jay, Maine — are redistributing capital toward established PJM and Mid-Atlantic corridors, tightening queue competition in precisely the markets where new groundbreakings are accelerating. Nuclear early-works agreements (Elementl Power/GE Vernova Hitachi) and SPAC closings (Hecate Energy) indicate that long-duration, capital-intensive power strategies are now moving from term sheet to execution, compressing the advisory engagement window for developers who have not yet locked in grid positions. Across qualified leads, a common operational gap persists: capital has been committed or announced, but interconnection queue strategy, PPA structuring, and site-level power origination remain unresolved or underpublicized. Developers and operators entering or expanding in ERCOT, PJM, MISO, and NYISO simultaneously face queue reform uncertainty, making third-party advisory support structurally more valuable than at any prior cycle.
