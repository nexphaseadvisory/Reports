Bottom Line
The U.S. data center power market is experiencing a simultaneous acceleration across every development stage: greenfield campus approvals, large-scale financing closes, nuclear early-works agreements, and fresh interconnection filings are all concentrating within a narrow 30-day window ending June 25, 2026. FERC's formal push to create expedited large-load interconnection pathways is reshaping queue strategy in real time, raising the premium on advisors who can translate regulatory change into executable grid access plans. Regulatory backlash — municipal moratoriums in Colorado, zoning reversals in Ohio — is emerging as a structural constraint that threatens capital deployment timelines for developers who entered markets without stakeholder-hardened power strategies. The convergence of capital availability (Blue Owl, SPAC closings, state incentive programs) with constrained grid access is compressing the decision window for power origination: developers who do not lock interconnection positions in the next 60–90 days risk being queue-gated through 2027–2028. Hyperscale lease signings at Applied Digital and Galaxy Digital's ERCOT-territory campus approval signal that demand-side commitments are running ahead of supply-side grid solutions across multiple ISOs.
