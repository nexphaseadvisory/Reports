Read  A moratorium at a 360MW brownfield conversion exposes the project to material schedule and capital risk, and the appeal process will require regulatory, community, and energy-siting strategy that Atmosphere's real-estate-centric team is not demonstrably equipped to execute. ISO New England
ISO New England filed an informational report with FERC proposing a 'bring your own generation' rule for large and co-located loads in response to a FERC show cause order.
Airtable  Open record
Read  A 'bring your own generation' standard would make incremental generation procurement a prerequisite for large load interconnection in New England, fundamentally shifting the advisory value chain for data center developers in the region toward PPA origination and generation-side interconnection structuring. This rule, if finalized, raises the barrier to entry for developers without sophisticated power procurement capabilities.
Strategic Thought  ISO-NE's proposed framework represents a structural shift in how grid operators are responding to large load growth, moving from passive interconnection processing toward active demand that new loads internalize their generation impact. For the advisory market, this creates a durable demand signal for PPA structuring, generation interconnection coordination, and ISO tariff navigation across every affected New England data center project. If other ISOs and RTOs follow ISO-NE's lead — as FERC's show cause order suggests is a policy direction — the 'bring your own generation' model could become a national template, materially expanding the market for integrated interconnection and power-origination advisory.
