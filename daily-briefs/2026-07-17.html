Bottom Line
Hyperscaler-driven data center demand continues to outpace secured power capacity across multiple U.S. markets, with new campuses in Texas and Virginia surfacing alongside announced signings in Dallas-Fort Worth — underscoring that power procurement and interconnection structuring remain the critical bottleneck in the development lifecycle. The Coravel brand launch and its reported ~140MW first hyperscaler customer signing marks a notable inflection point for a newly public platform that now carries real execution risk on the power side. Edge and distributed-node models are also entering the market, broadening the universe of operators who will need structured power solutions at scale. Internationally, Japanese GPU cloud operators are actively partnering for energy infrastructure, though U.S. ISO/RTO-specific expertise has limited direct applicability there. Across the board, the common thread is that development ambition is racing ahead of power certainty, creating sustained demand for origination and interconnection advisory capability.

This model creates demand for advisory capability that can design scalable power procurement templates rather than site-specific bespoke agreements, a distinct and less commoditized service niche. As edge computing footprints grow, the ability to structure power agreements that work across multiple ISOs, RTOs, and vertically integrated utility territories simultaneously will become a meaningful differentiator in the origination advisory market.
