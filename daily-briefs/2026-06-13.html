Issue No. 528 · US Data Center Power
The morning read on power-constrained demand.
21 developments worth a power developer’s attention today, ranked by where an independent originator can actually create value.
Bottom Line
Today's signals cluster around two reinforcing themes: secured power is now the explicit investment thesis driving institutional capital into data centers (KKR/Helix, Blue Owl/Corscale), and community opposition is emerging as a legitimate permitting risk across multiple geographies simultaneously (Nashville, Utah, Indiana, New York). Virginia and Utah are absorbing the most new campus proposals, putting Dominion and Rocky Mountain Power interconnection queues under compounding pressure. The O'Leary/Wonder Valley and Kenwood Commons signals both represent early-stage greenfield projects in non-traditional markets where interconnection naivety is highest and the window to influence the approach is still open. The macro read is clear: power certainty is replacing land as the scarce commodity, and whoever controls interconnection positions and bankable PPA structures controls the development pipeline.
