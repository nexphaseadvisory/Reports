Bottom Line
Today's signals confirm two converging macro forces: hyperscale AI demand is pulling capital into secondary markets (Missouri, Indiana, Ohio) faster than transmission infrastructure can keep pace, and sophisticated renewable developers are closing multi-billion-dollar financings on projects where Phase N+1 queue strategy is already the critical path item. The Champlain-Hudson activation is a structural shift in NYISO Zone J economics that will ripple through capacity market pricing and reshape the PPA calculus for every load-serving entity and IPP in the Con Ed zone. Offshore wind's continued retreat — Invenergy now exiting US leases — is freeing experienced development teams and balance sheet capacity that will redeploy into onshore storage and solar, compressing timelines in PJM and MISO queues. Meanwhile, first-time developers like TerraSpark are attempting 1.6-GW greenfield builds in complex PJM territory with no visible interconnection or offtake infrastructure, which is exactly where the gap between ambition and execution is widest.
