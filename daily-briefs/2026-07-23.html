Bottom Line
Capital deployment into AI and renewable energy infrastructure reached an inflection point this week, with a $40B data center acquisition, multiple greenfield campus approvals, and active site-scouting by a major AI-native hyperscaler all signaling that power procurement and interconnection advisory demand is accelerating across every major ISO region. The constraint layer has shifted decisively from capital availability to execution capability — specifically, the ability to originate power, navigate interconnection queues, and structure offtake under compressed timelines. Renewable developers commissioning large-scale solar while simultaneously breaking ground on multi-hundred-megawatt follow-on projects face equivalent complexity, as project financing closes demand immediate operationalization of power strategy. Water, permitting, and grid access are emerging as co-equal bottlenecks alongside interconnection, as illustrated by the IVCM lawsuit in California's Imperial Valley. The market is rewarding advisors with deep ISO/RTO fluency and PPA structuring experience, and penalizing organizations that treat power as a downstream procurement task rather than a front-end strategic function.

Read  A portfolio of this scale — 6.4GW across 51 campuses — does not operate on a single power strategy; each campus represents a discrete interconnection, PPA, and grid-integration workstream that must be managed concurrently across multiple ISOs. The ownership transition to financial sponsors with aggressive expansion mandates typically accelerates procurement timelines and creates demand for external advisory capacity that incumbent operators may not carry in-house.
Strategic Thought  Multi-GW hyperscale portfolio acquisitions are restructuring the power origination market by concentrating massive, geographically distributed load under single ownership groups that must procure at scale across ERCOT, PJM, WECC, and SPP simultaneously. This dynamic elevates the value of advisors who can operate across ISO jurisdictions rather than within a single market, and who can structure PPAs and interconnection agreements that are financeable under sponsor-grade underwriting standards. The $5B expansion tranche signals that interconnection queue filings, land and substation site control, and offtake origination will all be live workstreams in the near term.

The PJM interconnection process and Dominion's distribution planning cycle operate on timelines that do not compress to match developer ambitions, making pre-filing advisory engagement particularly valuable at the moment of land-use approval rather than after. Advisors with Dominion-specific interconnection experience and PJM queue fluency are directly positioned for engagement across all four Petersburg projects.

Advisors who can bridge between novel clean firm power developers and sophisticated hyperscale offtakers are positioned to capture value on both sides of these transactions.
