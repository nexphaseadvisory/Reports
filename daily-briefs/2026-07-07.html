Bottom Line
The AI/HPC datacenter buildout continues to accelerate across multiple ISOs, with anchor tenants such as Anthropic committing to 20-year lease structures that place enormous pressure on developers to deliver grid-connected capacity at scale. Developers ranging from crypto-to-AI converts to foreign entrants are discovering that power procurement and interconnection depth are the binding constraints on project velocity. ERCOT, PJM, and TVA-served territories are all seeing simultaneous queue congestion and developer activity, compressing the window between site control and shovel-ready status. The advisory gap between what internal teams can absorb and what programmatic multi-hundred-megawatt pipelines demand is widening measurably. Firms that close that gap fastest will capture anchor tenant economics before competing sites reach interconnection readiness.

Read  A 20-year anchor of this magnitude transforms TeraWulf from a crypto miner into a long-duration AI infrastructure landlord overnight, but it also exposes the gap between their existing power infrastructure and the sustained, high-reliability load profile Anthropic's operations require. Strategic Thought  This deal signals that hyperscalers are willing to commit to extremely long contract durations — 20 years — to secure dedicated capacity, which shifts the entire power procurement equation toward long-tenor PPAs and behind-the-meter solutions that can guarantee uptime commitments at that time horizon. Developers and operators without sophisticated PPA origination and interconnection advisory relationships will struggle to credibly underwrite 20-year SLA obligations to Anthropic-tier tenants. The Kentucky deal will likely catalyze competing long-duration lease discussions across PJM and SERC, tightening the advisory market for anyone who can structure power agreements to match hyperscaler lease durations.

Strategic Thought  TVA-served territory operates outside the FERC Order 2023 open-access framework that governs PJM and ERCOT interconnection, meaning developers entering that footprint must navigate a utility-specific large-load interconnection process with fewer standardized procedural guardrails — a dynamic that increases the value of advisors who have transacted in that specific utility relationship. Alabama is also emerging as a lower-cost alternative to saturated Virginia and Texas markets, which is likely to attract additional foreign and domestic developers who similarly lack TVA-specific power procurement expertise. The combination of market novelty, developing-sophistication operators, and a utility structure that rewards relationship capital creates a concentrated advisory opportunity as the Alabama datacenter market matures.
