26 developments worth a power developer’s attention today, ranked by where an independent originator can actually create value.
Bottom Line
Today's signals confirm two converging macro forces: first-of-kind nuclear technologies (SMR, advanced fission, microreactors) are crossing from design into physical execution, compressing the timeline for interconnection and offtake structuring from 'someday' to 'now'; and capital is flooding into large-load data center development at sites where grid access, community acceptance, and regulatory sophistication are the real rate-limiters, not compute ambition. The FERC large-load interconnection order is the regulatory backdrop tying all of this together — it signals that queue rules are actively being rewritten, which creates both risk and leverage for anyone with deep RTO process knowledge. Origis's $900M raise and Elementl's 1.5 GW Ohio SMR together illustrate the two dominant capital flows: experienced IPPs scaling pipelines aggressively, and nuclear developers betting that hyperscaler offtake demand is real and durable. The geographic spread — Wyoming, Kansas, Indiana, Ohio, California, Alberta — underscores that this is not a coastal phenomenon; the grid access constraint is national.
