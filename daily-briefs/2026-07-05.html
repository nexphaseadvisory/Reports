Bottom Line
The data center development landscape is bifurcating sharply between projects advancing through regulatory clarity and those stalled by municipal opposition, zoning reversals, or active litigation. Karis Cold's withdrawal of its Hoffman Estates rezoning request, Eagan Capital's legal challenge to a Minnesota moratorium, and Black Chamber's parcel assembly adjacent to a confirmed AWS campus each illustrate distinct inflection points where site strategy and power origination expertise carry decisive value. At the same time, Boulder City's failed referendum petition signals that developer-friendly council authority can persist even under community pressure, keeping certain markets open for rapid execution. Power queue constraints in PJM, ERCOT, and WECC continue to define competitive positioning, making interconnection advisory a critical path dependency rather than an ancillary service. The market continues to reward operators who can move from site control to interconnection agreement without regulatory attrition.

04  Market Signals · context, ranked by relevance
1
Boulder City  NEVADA
A Boulder City, NV residents' petition to require a public vote on data center approvals failed, leaving the city council with unilateral authority to approve data center projects.
Read  The failed referendum removes a significant permitting risk vector for developers targeting Boulder City, preserving the municipality as an accessible entry point into the Nevada power market. Developers can now proceed through standard council approval channels without the uncertainty of a public ballot trigger.
Strategic Thought  Nevada's data center development pipeline — particularly in NV Energy territory — remains viable for groups that can move quickly on interconnection without absorbing referendum delay risk. The Boulder City outcome reinforces that jurisdictional permitting risk is asymmetric across Nevada markets, and that power origination strategy must account for municipality-specific political dynamics, not just grid infrastructure constraints. Advisors with NV Energy interconnection fluency are positioned to add value immediately as developers assess whether Boulder City's open regulatory posture offsets its transmission limitations.

This dynamic is more pronounced in constrained UK grid markets where new interconnection capacity carries long lead times and ownership continuity matters to network operators.
