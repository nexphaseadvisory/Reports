Bottom Line
The data center and clean energy development landscape continues to surface regulatory friction, capital events, and land assembly activity as primary inflection points demanding rapid advisory response. Municipal moratoriums, rezoning withdrawals, and SPAC closings are compressing decision windows for developers and energy platforms that lack dedicated interconnection and power-origination capacity. Power queue complexity in MISO, PJM, ERCOT, and WECC remains the dominant structural bottleneck across active and emerging markets, with no near-term relief visible from ISO reform timelines. Capital-stage transitions — from pre-development to secured or construction — consistently expose organizational gaps in power strategy that external advisory can address on an accelerated basis. The confluence of regulatory, capital, and land signals across this week's queue reinforces that the advisory market opportunity is concentrated around companies facing irreversible timeline pressure.
