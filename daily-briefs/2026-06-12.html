Bottom Line
Today's signals converge on a single thesis: capital is flooding into AI data center infrastructure faster than power can be procured, and the bottleneck is shifting from land and financing to interconnection queue position and executable PPAs. The Dominion/PJM Northern Virginia corridor remains ground zero, with multiple independent developers stacking proposals in Goochland County alone, signaling that the next wave of NoVA capacity is moving west along the West Creek corridor. Community opposition and permitting moratoriums in Tennessee, Utah, and West Virginia are emerging as a real development tax that separates sophisticated operators from aspirational ones. KKR's $10B Helix launch with 'secured power' as its explicit founding thesis is the clearest institutional validation yet that power origination is now a primary asset — not a downstream afterthought. The UNKNOWN-sophistication greenfield projects in WV, Utah, and Romania represent the highest-probability engagement targets for advisory origination work over the next 30–60 days.
