Bottom Line
The AI/HPC datacenter build-out cycle continues to accelerate across ERCOT, PJM, Midwest, and international markets, with hyperscale tenants committing to decade-scale capacity agreements and first-time entrants racing to secure sites without the in-house power origination or interconnection expertise those commitments demand. Behind-the-meter and grid-scale power procurement complexity is intensifying simultaneously, as BTM portfolios scale into multi-gigawatt territory while grid queues in ERCOT and PJM remain among the most congested in North America. Crypto-to-AI pivot activity is broadening the pool of undercapitalized entrants who control real assets but lack structured power and interconnection capability. On the qualified-lead side, three companies spanning Texas ERCOT development, a multi-gigawatt BTM platform, and a cold-storage rezoning setback are all at inflection points that create near-term advisory entry windows. Market conditions broadly favor advisory engagements that can compress the gap between site control and commercial power certainty.
