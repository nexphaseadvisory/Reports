Bottom Line
The data center and power infrastructure market is operating under sustained capital pressure, with multiple large-scale financing events closing in rapid succession across PJM, ERCOT, MISO, and WECC — signaling that interconnection queue positioning and power origination strategy are now the primary execution constraints for developers carrying committed capital. Nuclear and early-works agreements are accelerating as developers seek long-duration power certainty, as demonstrated by Elementl Power's simultaneous site acquisition, PJM filing, and GE Vernova Hitachi partnership. Hyperscalers and grid operators are converging on flexibility-for-speed frameworks that will structurally reward developers and advisors who can translate load flexibility commitments into bankable interconnection agreements. Project withdrawals, such as Sentinel DC's exit from the Jay, Maine campus, are redirecting pipeline capital toward established ISO/RTO corridors in PJM and MISO, compressing the decision window for advisory engagement. The cumulative effect is a market where power origination and interconnection advisory services face peak demand precisely at the moment capital is deployed but grid access remains unresolved.

Read  A university-hosted data center project in Michigan entering the due diligence phase is at the earliest stage where power procurement strategy, utility coordination, and interconnection pathway analysis would be actively scoped — making it a potential early-entry advisory opening in MISO territory. Academic institutions sponsoring data center development typically lack in-house energy infrastructure expertise and rely on external advisory relationships formed during the due diligence phase.
Strategic Thought  University-sponsored data center projects entering formal due diligence represent a structurally underserved segment of the interconnection advisory market: they carry institutional credibility and access to capital, but lack the operational energy teams that private developers increasingly employ. In MISO-jurisdiction Michigan, where large-load interconnection study timelines and cost allocation mechanisms are growing in complexity, the due diligence phase is the most efficient entry point for establishing power origination and interconnection advisory relationships before RFP processes or utility pre-application meetings are initiated.
