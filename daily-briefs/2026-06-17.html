Bottom Line
Today's signals confirm that non-lithium LDES, BESS-integrated interconnection, and hyperscaler-driven load growth are the three dominant capital vectors reshaping queue strategy across every ISO. Sophisticated IPPs like MN8 and Cypress Creek are executing at scale with in-house horsepower, signaling that the market is bifurcating between operators who own the full stack and developers who are still assembling it. The ITC safe harbor deadline is forcing creative and legally fragile procurement decisions across the developer community, compressing timelines and elevating interconnection sequencing risk industry-wide. Coal retirements, emergency grid orders, and utility capex pressure are collectively widening the reliability gap that firm renewable and storage assets will be asked to fill. The convergence of modular data center construction, alternative battery chemistries, and state-level BTM storage incentives suggests the next 18 months will stress-test every queue position and PPA structure in the pipeline.
