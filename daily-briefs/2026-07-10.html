Bottom Line
Industrial-to-data-center land conversions are accelerating across constrained ISO markets, with CAISO and ERCOT seeing the most active repositioning as developers race to lock in power before interconnection queues close further. Regulatory friction — illustrated by the Indianapolis project reduction — is emerging as a co-equal risk factor alongside power availability, signaling that community and siting navigation is becoming inseparable from power procurement strategy. Renewables developers with existing grid relationships, such as Belltown Power, are vertically integrating into data center development, compressing the margin for pure-play advisory if they build sufficient in-house origination depth. Meanwhile, MISO capacity scarcity continues to pressure developers holding live load obligations without secured interconnection paths, keeping that market acutely exposed. The overall signal is a market tightening on all three axes — land, power, and permitting — simultaneously.

Prologis  CALIFORNIA
Prologis has filed plans to develop a 99 MW data center on a San Jose industrial site previously designated for warehouse use, marking a deliberate pivot of land assets into compute infrastructure inside the CAISO footprint.
Airtable  Open record
Read  A 99 MW conversion in a constrained CAISO market immediately raises unsolved questions around interconnection point selection, PPA structuring, and utility coordination — functions that a traditional industrial REIT is unlikely to have built in-house. The filing signals urgency, as entitlement timelines in CAISO are long and queue positions are scarce.
Strategic Thought  Industrial owners converting land to data center use in constrained ISO markets represent a structurally underserved segment for interconnection and power origination advisory, because their core competency is real estate, not grid access. A 99 MW load in CAISO requires navigating one of the most complex and congested interconnection queues in the country, and the absence of a power-native team inside a REIT creates a durable advisory gap. This signal reinforces a broader pattern of non-utility, non-developer entrants needing to acquire power expertise either through hiring or external engagement.

Provident Data Centers  INDIANA
Provident Data Centers reduced the scale of its east-side Indianapolis data center project days before a final city council vote, responding to community opposition.
Airtable  Open record
Read  Late-stage project scope reductions driven by local pushback are becoming a distinct project risk category, separate from — but compounding — power procurement challenges. For a developer, scaling back megawatts also disrupts load forecasting, PPA sizing, and interconnection application parameters, requiring procurement strategy to be re-run.
Strategic Thought  Community opposition-driven scope changes in data center development are creating a secondary advisory need: the ability to remodel power procurement and interconnection applications mid-cycle when MW commitments shift. This is distinct from initial origination work and represents a gap that developers without embedded power advisory support are poorly positioned to fill quickly. The Indiana market, while outside ERCOT and CAISO, is a MISO-adjacent signal that regulatory friction is now a national pattern rather than an ISO-specific phenomenon.

A Belltown Power affiliate has filed plans for a 100,000 square foot data center in Royse City, Texas, targeting a next-year launch and marking the renewables developer's move into ERCOT data center development.
Airtable  Open record
Read  Belltown's vertical integration from renewable development into data center ownership is notable because the company brings established interconnection and power origination capabilities that most data center developers lack, reducing the advisory gap that typically exists at this project stage. The ERCOT location and accelerated timeline suggest Belltown is leveraging existing grid relationships to compress what is typically a multi-year interconnection process.
Strategic Thought  When sophisticated renewable developers with mature in-house origination teams enter data center development, they narrow the addressable market for external power advisory on a project-by-project basis, but they simultaneously validate the thesis that power expertise is the critical path asset in this sector. The broader market implication is bifurcation: developers with grid lineage move faster and require less advisory support, while real estate, financial, and non-energy entrants accumulate structural deficits that external expertise is well-positioned to fill. Tracking which ERCOT data center entrants lack Belltown's grid background is the relevant segmentation lens.

While this specific signal is outside U.S. ISO/RTO markets, it reflects the same dynamic driving advisory demand in ERCOT, CAISO, and MISO: developers need grid expertise at the moment of project formation, not as a retrofit. The BESS component is a leading indicator of complexity, as storage-coupled projects require interconnection treatment, storage operating agreements, and market participation decisions that compound origination risk.
