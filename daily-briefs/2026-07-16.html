Bottom Line
The data center power market is entering a phase of compounding structural complexity: gigawatt-scale BTM campuses are accelerating in ERCOT, regulated cost-allocation battles are intensifying in Virginia, and greenfield developers are closing multi-billion-dollar financings before offtake and interconnection strategies are fully resolved. The Steel River Energy Center's $3.5B financial close and Phase 3 offtake openness exemplify a recurring dynamic where construction momentum outpaces power-origination strategy, creating a narrow and high-value advisory window. Regulatory pressure in PJM-adjacent markets and SPP is also rising, as utilities and commissions scrutinize how large-load interconnection costs are socialized across ratepayer classes. Developers and large-load buyers who fail to structure interconnection and offtake agreements with explicit cost-allocation provisions face growing regulatory and financial exposure. The convergence of accelerating build timelines, queue complexity, and shifting cost-allocation rules elevates the urgency of specialized interconnection and power-origination advisory across all major ISO/RTO markets.
