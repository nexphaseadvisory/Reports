Bottom Line
The data center power origination market is accelerating on multiple fronts simultaneously, with large-scale financing closes, ground breakings, and site acquisitions compressing the window between announcement and construction-ready status. Texas and Virginia remain the dominant origination battlegrounds, while industrial site conversions in secondary markets are emerging as a distinct subsegment requiring bespoke interconnection structuring. Crypto-native operators pivoting to AI and hyperscale tenants represent a structurally undersophisticated cohort on power procurement despite their operational scale, creating recurring advisory gaps. Green debt financing at the multi-billion-dollar level is now a standard capital structure for Tier 1 digital infrastructure developers, elevating the complexity and strategic importance of the underlying PPA and interconnection architecture. The pace of CUP filings, site acquisitions, and financing announcements in June 2026 confirms that the market is in an active deployment phase, not a planning phase.
