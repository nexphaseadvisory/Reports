Bottom Line
The AI-driven data center buildout continues to overwhelm interconnection queues across PJM, ERCOT, and WECC, with capital commitments accelerating faster than grid capacity can be permitted or energized. Regulatory friction is emerging as a primary constraint: Eagan Capital's Minnesota lawsuit illustrates how local moratorium risk is now a transaction-level variable for data center developers, while National Grid's $1.75B Joulent investment signals that institutional capital is pricing interconnection delay as a systemic problem requiring structural solutions. On the generation side, novel power architectures — gas-bridge-to-nuclear, BTM microreactors, and multi-GW greenfield campuses — are moving from concept to early-stage procurement, compressing the window for origination advisors to establish positioning. Meanwhile, AD/CVD enforcement on Southeast Asian PV modules is introducing new supply-chain fragility into solar development timelines, adding another variable to interconnection scheduling and PPA obligation risk.
