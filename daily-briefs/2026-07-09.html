Bottom Line
The data center development pipeline continues to accelerate across multiple U.S. markets, with new permit filings, campus-scale expansions, and emerging-market entries signaling sustained demand for power origination and interconnection expertise. Regulatory friction is emerging as a material risk, with municipal-level restrictions in Nashville and a moratorium environment in Kansas City creating project-specific permitting complexity that advisory capacity can help navigate. ERCOT, PJM, and regional utility markets are each seeing fresh large-load entrants, compressing the window between permit filing and interconnection queue positioning. Developer sophistication varies widely — from institutional-scale players restructuring multi-hundred-megawatt campuses to first-time regional operators entering unfamiliar utility territories. The cumulative signal is a market where power strategy and regulatory navigation are becoming as critical as site selection.
