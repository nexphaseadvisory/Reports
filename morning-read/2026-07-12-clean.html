01  The Bottom Line
Two stories dominate today: the NextEra-Dominion megamerger aimed squarely at Data Center Alley, and EQT's acquisition of Copia Power from Carlyle. Both are capital-scale moves that reprice queue positions and origination relationships in PJM's most contested corridors. The state-level policy signal running underneath, from North Carolina's electricity tax repeal to New Jersey's transmission oversight push to Florida's large-load tariff fight, is that the subsidy and cost-allocation free ride for hyperscale load is ending at every level of government simultaneously.
02  Signal of the Day
National & Policy
EQT acquires energy and data center developer Copia Power from Carlyle.
EQT, Copia Power, Carlyle · EQT · 2026-07-12
The Read  EQT acquiring Copia Power from Carlyle reorders the developer landscape. Copia had been building a book of renewable and data center development projects. Why Carlyle sold now is not stated. What changes is the sponsor: EQT brings balance sheet depth that a fund structure does not, meaning projects that need capital to clear late-cycle study costs now have a different kind of owner. Watch queue amendments and site announcements as EQT signals which assets it intends to push to completion versus rationalize.

01
PJM status quo ‘untenable’: FERC Commissioner LaCerte
“Looking at things differently is hard, especially in entrenched utilities that have been doing this for a long time,” the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission member said.
Utility Dive · 2026-07-12
The Read  When a sitting FERC commissioner calls PJM's status quo untenable in a public forum, that's not neutral commentary. And LaCerte isn't alone. Chairman Swett has been separately questioning whether PJM is "too big to function." What they're signaling is appetite for real structural intervention on governance and the stakeholder process, not incremental rule tweaks. So if you hold interconnection or transmission positions in PJM, the thing to watch is whether FERC moves on process reform, transmission incentives, or both. The sequencing is what decides which positions are at risk and which ones gain value.

Base Power brings cheap batteries to residents in power-starved PJM
PJM Interconnection, which serves 67 million people across 13 states from the mid-Atlantic to the Midwest, has become a poster child for how not to keep up with soaring energy demand. Startup Base Power is taking a whack at that problem by installing a network of unusually large home batteries in one corner of that…
Canary Media · 2026-07-12
The Read  Base Power's model is a queue bypass: residential interconnections already exist, the battery goes in behind the meter, and PJM's aggregation rules do the rest. The 40 kWh unit at $95 upfront is a customer acquisition play funded by the wholesale market optionality the fleet creates. Why customers agree to an ongoing retail electricity relationship is clear from pricing alone. At scale this is dispatchable capacity that never touched the interconnection queue, which is exactly what ComEd's constrained territory needs. The constraint is time: building a grid-relevant fleet battery by battery is slow, and the capacity market signal is already ringing.
National & Policy  · 5 signals
01
North Carolina repealed sales tax exemption on data center electricity while preserving equipment incentives, raising ~$21.4M in FY26-27.
North Carolina · North Carolina · 2026-07-12
The Read  North Carolina just told data center operators that the operating subsidy is gone even as the capital incentive survives. The fiscal impact is modest at the state level, but the policy direction is not: lawmakers are separating 'you built here' from 'you run here forever at subsidized rates.' For operators underwriting long-run operating costs on a North Carolina campus, the electricity cost stack just moved, and the magnitude grows each year through 2031. Sites already under LOI need to rerun their power cost models before executing.
02
Florida PSC Advances Duke Energy’s Large Load Tariff Proposal
Duke Energy faced skepticism at the Florida PSC meeting over whether its application for a large load tariff complies with a recently enacted state law aimed at ensuring that new data centers shoulder the full cost of meeting their power needs.
RTO Insider · 2026-07-12
The Read  Florida PSC advancing Duke's large-load tariff proposal while expressing doubt about statutory compliance is a procedurally unstable outcome. A tariff that clears a commission vote but carries a compliance cloud invites rehearing or legal challenge before any hyperscaler customer signs on. Watch whether the commission issues a compliance order or whether the preemption question gets kicked to FERC.
03
New Jersey increases transmission oversight in ‘affordability’ push
New Jersey’s governor signed bills that aim to cut transmission owners’ return on equity, establish data center tariffs and require state approval for “supplementary” transmission
Utility Dive · 2026-07-12
The Read  New Jersey is now legislating on three fronts simultaneously: capping transmission owner ROE, mandating data center tariffs, and requiring state approval for supplementary transmission builds. That last piece is the one to watch. State approval requirements layered on top of PJM's planning process create a new veto point that transmission developers have not had to navigate in New Jersey before. Any merchant transmission or upgrade project touching New Jersey interconnection rights just got a longer permitting horizon.
