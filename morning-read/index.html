01  The Bottom Line
Two signals dominate this morning's issue: Meta's Louisiana campus now stands at 5GW and roughly $50B in committed capital, which is the largest single-site hyperscaler load commitment in MISO history and will reprice every interconnection position in that region. Simultaneously, Texas just handed data centers a new grid-participation mandate with its ride-through rules, and Nashville froze permits while zoning catches up. The pattern is consistent: load is winning the race against policy, and jurisdictions that have not drawn the line yet are drawing it now.

Data CentersPA Data Center Partners filed data center plans in Granville Township, Mifflin County, PA two days before local moratorium vote.The Read▾
PA Data Center Partners · 2026-07-13
Racing the clock on a moratorium is a well-worn tactic, and PA Data Center Partners executed it cleanly. Filing two days before the curative amendment vote locks the proposal into the current ordinance, which has no data center definition and therefore no restrictive standards to apply. Whether that legal position holds through an appeal is the real question. Mifflin County is not Loudoun, but the community opposition pattern here looks like every rural Pennsylvania township that got surprised by a large industrial use. Developers watching PJM's western reaches for land should understand that moratorium risk is not just a suburban Virginia problem anymore.

Meta · 2026-07-13
Five gigawatts at a single campus in Richland Parish is not a data center story, it is a grid transformation story for MISO South. The $50B commitment means Meta is not leasing capacity here: it owns the demand and it will own the interconnection process. Every nearby generation developer, transmission planner, and load-serving entity in Louisiana needs to treat this as the load event that resets their assumptions about transmission headroom, capacity pricing, and fuel supply in the region. The interconnection queue filings that correspond to this load will be the most important documents MISO processes this cycle. Find them.

02PolicyTexas PUC approves 'ride-through' rules requiring data centers to withstand voltage and frequency excursions to protect grid reliability.The Read▾
Public Utility Commission of Texas · 2026-07-13
The Texas PUC's ride-through rules are consequential for anyone structuring a large behind-the-meter load in ERCOT. Requiring data centers to withstand voltage and frequency excursions without tripping is a reliability mandate that shifts design cost onto the load, not the grid. For developers already in the queue with standard UPS and generator assumptions, the equipment specifications may need to be revisited. This is a real compliance cost, and it will show up in the next round of data center lease economics in Texas.
