01  The Bottom Line
Two stories define today: EQT's acquisition of Copia Power confirms that the co-located generation-plus-data-center model has graduated from concept to institutional asset class, and TeraWulf's $3.5B debt raise on a 20-year Anthropic lease shows that AI offtake is now financeable at project-bond scale. Everything else in today's signals is downstream of those two facts. Developers without a credible power solution at the interconnection point are being priced out of the market by platforms that control both sides of the wire.

02
PJM Announces No PAI from Heat Wave Emergency Actions
PJM determined there was no performance assessment interval during the heat wave spanning from June 29 to July 5, which saw several emergency actions taken as dispatchers managed a record 168-GW unrestricted peak load.
RTO Insider · 2026-07-13
The Read  No PAI declared after a 168-GW peak with emergency actions in place. That outcome matters to capacity market participants: generators that underperformed during the event period avoid the performance penalty that a PAI would have triggered. The record peak itself is the number worth sitting with. PJM's unrestricted load is growing faster than the capacity additions needed to serve it, and the next heat event may not resolve as cleanly.
03
PJM status quo ‘untenable’: FERC Commissioner LaCerte
“Looking at things differently is hard, especially in entrenched utilities that have been doing this for a long time,” the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission member said.
Utility Dive · 2026-07-13
The Read  When a sitting FERC commissioner calls PJM's status quo untenable in a public forum, that's not neutral commentary. And LaCerte isn't alone. Chairman Swett has been separately questioning whether PJM is "too big to function." What they're signaling is appetite for real structural intervention on governance and the stakeholder process, not incremental rule tweaks. So if you hold interconnection or transmission positions in PJM, the thing to watch is whether FERC moves on process reform, transmission incentives, or both. The sequencing is what decides which positions are at risk and which ones gain value.

02
TeraWulf seeking $3.5bn debt led by Morgan Stanley to build Anthropic-leased data center at Justified Data campus in Hawesville, Kentucky
TeraWulf · TeraWulf · 2026-07-11
The Read  A $3.5B project financing backed by a 20-year Anthropic lease with an estimated $19B total contract value is the clearest reference transaction yet for AI lab offtake crossing into investment-grade project finance. Morgan Stanley leading with high-yield bonds means the market is pricing Anthropic's covenant quality as sufficient collateral without a utility counterparty behind it. Lenders underwriting data center project finance should treat this deal's capital stack as the new benchmark.
03
Former FERC officials warn Supreme Court ruling expanding presidential power to fire regulators threatens consumer protections and regulatory independence.
FERC · 2026-07-11
The Read  FERC commissioner independence has always been a structural assumption underneath every long-dated interconnection agreement and tariff filing. If that assumption weakens materially, the risk premium on regulatory outcomes shifts and counterparties will want shorter commitment windows. This is worth watching at the appellate level before treating it as a current transaction risk.
04
OBBBA has wiped out $68.2B+ in US clean energy capital investment, per E2 analysis, signaling major policy disruption for renewable developers.
E2 (Environmental Entrepreneurs) · E2 (Environmental Entrepreneurs) · 2026-07-11
The Read  The E2 analysis documents what the industry has been tracking since November: OBBBA accelerated cancellations that were already in motion after the election, and battery storage took the largest proportional hit. For developers with active PJM or MISO queues on storage-heavy projects, the financing environment has tightened materially regardless of whether the underlying resource is still economic. The $68B figure is cumulative disruption, not a single event, and the annual GDP drag compounds as operating projects stay cancelled. Any merchant storage position that was underwritten on ITC or PTC assumptions from 2024 needs to be revisited against current law.
