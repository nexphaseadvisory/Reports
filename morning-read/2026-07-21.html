03  Market Intelligence
PJM  · 1 signal
01PolicyPJM’s old way of getting power built isn’t working. Has it found a fix?Held for reviewThe Read▾
PJM Interconnection · 2026-07-17
PJM stakeholders recently voted to approve a reliability backstop procurement mechanism that would require load-serving entities for large new loads to directly procure the generation needed to serve them, rather than socializing that cost across all ratepayers. FERC approval is still required, and the protest window is where incumbent generators and state commissions, which have historically clashed over who bears new-build costs in PJM, could complicate the path to a final order. Any developer expecting to lean on PJM's existing capacity market to backstop a large load commitment should be revisiting that assumption now.
MISO  · 1 signal
01SignalMISO Presses Ahead with Zero-injection Gen AgreementsThe Read▾
MISO · 2026-07-19
MISO pressing ahead with zero-injection gen agreements despite stakeholder pushback is a practical acknowledgment that the grid cannot wait for consensus. These agreements let generation connect to the system without injecting power onto the grid, which is the mechanism hyperscalers and developers need to bring dedicated generation online without sitting in a full interconnection study queue. The one-month delay changed nothing substantive. Developers with MISO load positions should be tracking the filing date and understanding whether their behind-the-fence generation strategy qualifies for this treatment.

Pre-approved grid interconnection is the operative phrase, and it is the entire reason Lancium could activate two gigawatt-scale campuses in a single week.
