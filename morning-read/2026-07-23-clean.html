01  The Bottom Line
Two stories define today: PJM's capacity market is structurally broken at $325/MW-day for the third consecutive auction, and the White House is now threatening governance intervention on top of that, which means every developer underwriting PJM load growth on historical capacity cost assumptions needs to reprice. Away from PJM, the TeraWulf-Anthropic deal at $19B over 20 years is the clearest signal yet that frontier AI labs are willing to sign decade-scale anchor leases to control their compute supply chain, and that changes how greenfield campus economics pencil for any developer with a creditworthy anchor in hand.

PJM Interconnection · 2026-07-22
The White House warning PJM to reform governance before it is too late is political pressure with real regulatory teeth if FERC follows. The specific complaint, that PJM's board is controlled by companies that profit under rules they write, has been a structural criticism for years. What is new is the administration attaching urgency to it explicitly in the context of AI power demand, with 13-governor alignment across a region with fundamentally different politics. Whether FERC acts or waits for PJM to self-reform is the question that determines timing.

CAISO  · 2 signals
01Data CentersImperial Valley Computer Manufacturing sues IID after denial of 750,000 gpd water request for planned 330MW AI data center in Imperial County, CA.The Read▾
Imperial Valley Computer Manufacturing · 2026-07-22
This lawsuit is the most consequential water-rights case in U.S. data center siting right now. IVCM's theory, that retiring farmland and transferring its historical Colorado River allocation to industrial use should be legally straightforward, is exactly what the court will test. If IID's position holds, it closes that path and forces the broader Lithium Valley industrial plan to source water through a different mechanism. Anyone underwriting desert data center sites in California with a water sourcing strategy that depends on agricultural transfer should watch this case closely.

ISO-NE  · 1 signal
01PolicyISO-NE files informational report on FERC show cause order for large/co-located loads, proposing 'bring your own generation' rule for data centers.The Read▾
ISO New England · 2026-07-21
ISO-NE is proposing the cleanest version of bring-your-own-generation for large loads that any RTO has put on paper: new large loads must bring incremental generation and cannot procure capacity through the regional capacity market. The filing is an informational report, not a final rule, so the direction is set but not locked. For any developer eyeing New England sites, the interconnection strategy now has to include a paired generation solution, which raises capital requirements and development complexity materially.
