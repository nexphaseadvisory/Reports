01  The Bottom Line
The capital is committed and the customers are named: Anthropic at 401 MW in Kentucky, OpenAI contracting 3.2 GW from Georgia Power, xAI circling gigawatt-scale sites in Texas. The constraint is not demand or equity, it is the physical power stack, and ISO-NE's bring-your-own-generation proposal plus PJM's reliability backstop procurement are the two regulatory moves that will reshape how the next wave of load gets priced into interconnection strategy. Every deal that closes without a solved power position is a future renegotiation.

01PolicyWhite House warns PJM to reform its industry-controlled board and stakeholder process 'before it is too late' amid AI-driven power demand pressures.The Read▾
PJM Interconnection · 2026-07-22
The White House publicly warning PJM to reform its board governance or face consequences is a significant escalation, particularly given that PJM disputes the characterization that it has failed to act on the January Statement of Principles. The governance fight matters to power market participants because board composition drives the rules on cost allocation, interconnection policy, and capacity market design. If the administration follows through with external pressure, whether through FERC, congressional action, or direct engagement with state governors, the stakeholder process that produced last month's reliability backstop procurement approval could be disrupted before the FERC filing lands. Watch the timing of that submission against any White House escalation.

Gigawatt-scale xAI sites near Austin, Bastrop, and Conroe are all ERCOT territory, and the constraint in each of those areas is not land. ISO-NE  · 1 signal
01PolicyISO-NE files informational report on FERC show cause order for large/co-located loads, proposing 'bring your own generation' rule for data centers.The Read▾
ISO New England · 2026-07-21
ISO-NE's bring-your-own-generation proposal is the most consequential policy development in New England grid access in years. The rule as outlined would bar large new loads from procuring capacity through the regional capacity market, meaning a data center developer in ISO-NE cannot simply pay the Forward Capacity Auction clearing price and call the power problem solved. They would need to bring incremental generation into the interconnection queue and tie it to their load. That fundamentally changes the origination model for New England sites: the land, the generation, and the load connection become a single integrated transaction rather than three separable decisions. The 90-day abeyance request and the November 16 Section 205 filing deadline are the dates to track.
