01  The Bottom Line
Held for review
The locational vise is tightening on data center development from every direction at once: Virginia kills projects on community backlash, Prince George's County imposes a moratorium, New Jersey rewrites the tariff and capacity rules, Nashville moves to restrict, and Oregon raises rates 29%. Developers who treated site control as the hard part are learning that permitting, tariff treatment, and grid cost allocation are now equally constraining. The queue position and the power deal still matter, but the regulatory path to operating has become the critical path.
