01  The Bottom Line
Held for review
The story today is not the volume of filings; it is the regulatory friction closing in from every direction. Virginia kills two major campuses in quick succession, Prince George's County drops a two-year moratorium, New Jersey layers capacity obligation rules and transmission controls, Oregon passes interconnection cost-shifting onto data centers, and Alabama opens its own review. Developers who treated permitting and tariff risk as secondary to queue position need to reprice that assumption fast, because the political environment is now as rate-limiting as the interconnection queue itself.
Editorial thesis, held pending review before send.

05
Oregon Regulators Approve PGE Data Center Rate Hike
The Oregon Public Utilities Commission approved Portland General Electric’s proposal to increase electricity rates for data centers by 29% and is utility’s response to a state mandate requiring the facilities bear most of their own interconnection costs.
RTO Insider · 2026-07-09
The Read  Oregon's 29% rate increase for data centers, structured as cost-shifting for interconnection expenses under a state mandate, is a template other state commissions will cite. The mechanism matters: it is not a general rate increase but a cost-causation argument that says large loads that drive interconnection upgrades should pay for them. That logic is durable and will survive regulatory challenge better than a pure revenue-need argument. Any data center developer in a state where the PUC has been watching Oregon should assume this is coming to their jurisdiction. The Data Center Coalition has already petitioned the PUC to reconsider, so the final structure is still in play.
