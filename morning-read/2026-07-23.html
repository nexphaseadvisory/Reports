01  The Bottom Line
Held for review
The market is sorting itself into two tiers in real time. Tier one is the hyperscaler and frontier-AI anchor tenant signing 20-year, multibillion-dollar leases and gigawatt-scale power contracts before the infrastructure exists. Tier two is everyone else scrambling for interconnection rights, water, and dispatchable capacity in a queue that cannot keep pace. PJM's capacity auction clearing at the price cap for the third consecutive time, combined with the White House threatening governance intervention, means the policy and market pressure on that RTO is now coming from multiple directions simultaneously, and developers sitting on firm interconnection rights in that footprint are holding more value than they may realize.
Editorial thesis, held pending review before send.

03  Market Intelligence
PJM  · 1 signal
01PolicyWhite House warns PJM to reform its industry-controlled board and stakeholder process 'before it is too late' amid AI-driven power demand pressures.The Read▾
PJM Interconnection · 2026-07-22
The White House publicly naming PJM's board governance as structurally conflicted and warning of action 'before it is too late' is not a routine policy statement. The administration is telling the largest RTO in the country that its current governance model is incompatible with the energy policy agenda, and it is doing so publicly while PJM's capacity auction is clearing at the price cap for the third consecutive time. PJM's position that it remains on the previously recommended timeline may be factually defensible, but it is politically untenable when 13 governors and the White House are aligned on the other side. Developers with long queue timelines in PJM should model governance reform scenarios; a board restructuring forced by FERC under Section 206 would reset stakeholder dynamics in ways that are hard to predict.

SpaceXAI · 2026-07-22
Gigawatt-scale xAI demand in Texas is the load event ERCOT's resource planners have to model, and the site selection between Austin, Bastrop, and Conroe will drive very different interconnection outcomes. Conroe puts the load in the Houston load zone with constrained transmission from west Texas wind. Bastrop is closer to existing data center build-out in the Austin corridor but faces similar westbound constraints. If this moves from talks to a filing, the queue position and load zone choice will be watched closely by every merchant generator and transmission developer in the ERCOT footprint.

ISO-NE  · 1 signal
01PolicyISO-NE files informational report on FERC show cause order for large/co-located loads, proposing 'bring your own generation' rule for data centers.The Read▾
ISO New England · 2026-07-21
ISO-NE's bring-your-own-generation proposal is a more aggressive structural response to the FERC show cause order than most RTOs have offered. Requiring new large loads to supply incremental generation and prohibiting them from procuring capacity through the regional market effectively walls off data centers from the existing capacity construct entirely. The 90-day abeyance request with a November 16 Section 205 filing deadline means stakeholders have a narrow window to shape whether this becomes the permanent rule. For any developer eyeing New England, the power supply requirement is now load-obligated, not capacity-market-accessible, and that changes the economics of every site in the region.

U.S. Energy Information Administration · 2026-07-21
Power sector CO2 up 4% in 2025 with coal generation rising 13% is the empirical record of what the data center demand surge actually did to the grid last year. The direction is clear: aggregate renewable additions are not offsetting the load growth fast enough to hold emissions flat, and coal is filling the gap. This matters for any developer or hyperscaler with CFE commitments, because the hourly matching math just got harder.
