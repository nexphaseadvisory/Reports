Rubix Data Centers · 2026-07-22
Two gigawatts and twelve billion dollars at a former AK Steel brownfield in Kentucky is exactly the kind of announcement that sounds transformative and means almost nothing until the power question is answered. The source is explicit: no power source is secured. At that scale, you are looking at a new bulk transmission interconnection in a territory that has not historically seen hyperscale data center load, and the study queue timeline for a 2GW interconnection request in that footprint will stretch years. Submer's Rubix is a credible company, but the gap between a brownfield site announcement and a cleared interconnection study at 2GW is where most projects at this scale quietly shrink or phase down. Watch whether an interconnection request actually gets filed, and in whose name.

02SignalFERC Rejects Mich. Muni’s Late Entry into MISO Capacity AuctionThe Read▾
FERC · 2026-07-22
Lansing Board of Water and Light bought capacity in MISO's 2026/27 auction at $400/MW-day and then got denied the late-entry correction they needed, leaving them holding duplicative capacity they now have to sell into a market at a price that will not cover what they paid. The procedural lesson is simple: municipal utilities running coal-to-gas conversions on a minority plant ownership stake need to have their capacity positions confirmed before the auction window closes, not after. FERC's denial here is consistent with how the commission has treated procedural deadlines in recent capacity market cases.
